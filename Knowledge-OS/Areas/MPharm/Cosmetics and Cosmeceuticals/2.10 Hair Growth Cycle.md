# Hair Growth Cycle (10 Marks)

## Introduction

Hair growth is a **cyclic process** in which hair follicles undergo repeated phases of growth, regression, rest, and shedding. Each hair follicle functions independently, ensuring continuous hair renewal throughout life.

---

# Hair Growth Cycle

The hair growth cycle consists of **4 phases**:

1. **Anagen Phase (Growth Phase)**
    
2. **Catagen Phase (Transition Phase)**
    
3. **Telogen Phase (Resting Phase)**
    
4. **Exogen Phase (Shedding Phase)**
    

---

## 1. Anagen Phase (Growth Phase)

### Definition

The active phase during which hair grows continuously due to rapid division of matrix cells.

### Features

- Hair shaft is formed.
    
- Matrix cells divide rapidly.
    
- Melanin production is active.
    
- Hair length increases continuously.
    

### Duration

- **2–7 years**
    
- About **85–90%** of scalp hairs are in this phase.
    

### Importance

- Determines maximum hair length.
    

---

## 2. Catagen Phase (Transition Phase)

### Definition

A short transitional phase between growth and rest.

### Features

- Hair growth stops.
    
- Hair follicle shrinks.
    
- Dermal papilla separates from hair bulb.
    
- Hair matrix becomes inactive.
    

### Duration

- **2–3 weeks**
    
- About **1–2%** of hairs.
    

---

## 3. Telogen Phase (Resting Phase)

### Definition

The resting stage of the hair follicle.

### Features

- No active hair growth.
    
- Hair remains attached as a **club hair**.
    
- Follicle remains inactive.
    

### Duration

- About **3 months**
    
- Approximately **10–15%** of hairs.
    

---

## 4. Exogen Phase (Shedding Phase)

### Definition

The phase in which old hair is shed and replaced by a new hair.

### Features

- Club hair falls out.
    
- New anagen hair starts growing.
    
- Normal hair loss occurs.
    

### Hair Loss

- **50–100 hairs/day** is considered normal.

---

# Significance of Hair Growth Cycle

- Maintains continuous hair renewal.
    
- Replaces old hairs with new hairs.
    
- Helps maintain normal hair density.
    
- Disturbance of the cycle may cause hair loss disorders such as **telogen effluvium** and **alopecia**.
    

---

# Conclusion

Hair growth occurs through a cyclic sequence of **Anagen (growth), Catagen (transition), Telogen (resting), and Exogen (shedding)** phases. Proper functioning of these phases is essential for maintaining healthy hair growth and normal hair density.
