# Regulatory Provisions Relating to Manufacture of Cosmetics: Conditions for Obtaining License, Prohibition of Manufacture and Sale of Certain Cosmetics, Loan License, Offences and Penalties (10 Marks)

## Introduction

The manufacture of cosmetics in India is regulated under the **Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940**, **Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945**, and **Cosmetics Rules, 2020**. These regulations ensure that cosmetics manufactured and marketed in India are safe, effective, and of standard quality.

---

# 1. Conditions for Obtaining a Cosmetic Manufacturing License

A manufacturing license is granted by the **State Licensing Authority (SLA)** after inspection of the manufacturing premises.

### A. Premises Requirements

The manufacturing unit must have:

- Adequate space and proper layout
    
- Separate areas for production, storage, packaging, and quality control
    
- Good ventilation and lighting
    
- Proper water supply and sanitation facilities
    
- Pest control measures
    

### B. Equipment Requirements

- Suitable manufacturing equipment
    
- Equipment made of non-reactive materials
    
- Proper cleaning and maintenance
    
- Calibration of instruments
    

### C. Qualified Technical Staff

The manufacturer must employ a competent technical person having qualifications in:

- Pharmacy
    
- Chemistry
    
- Microbiology
    
- Related pharmaceutical sciences
    

### D. Quality Control Facilities

A separate quality control laboratory should be available for:

- Physical testing
    
- Chemical testing
    
- Microbiological testing
    

### E. Documentation

- Batch manufacturing records
    
- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
    
- Raw material records
    
- Quality control records
    

must be maintained.

---

# 2. Prohibition of Manufacture and Sale of Certain Cosmetics

Under the Drugs and Cosmetics Act, manufacture or sale of the following cosmetics is prohibited:

### 1. Misbranded Cosmetics

Cosmetics with false, misleading, or incomplete labeling.

### 2. Spurious Cosmetics

Counterfeit cosmetics sold under another manufacturer's name.

### 3. Cosmetics Containing Prohibited Ingredients

Products containing banned or unsafe substances.

### 4. Cosmetics Not of Standard Quality

Products failing prescribed quality and safety standards.

### 5. Cosmetics Manufactured Without License

Any cosmetic manufactured without obtaining a valid license is prohibited.

---

# 3. Loan License

## Definition

A **Loan License** is a license issued to a person who does not own manufacturing facilities but intends to manufacture cosmetics using the facilities of another licensed manufacturer.

### Features

- Manufacturing is carried out in an already licensed factory.
    
- The loan license holder markets the product under his own brand name.
    
- Manufacturing facilities and quality control systems must meet regulatory requirements.
    
- Inspection by licensing authority is required before grant of license.
    

### Advantages

- Low investment.
    
- No need to establish own manufacturing unit.
    
- Faster entry into the cosmetic market.
    

---

# 4. Offences Related to Cosmetics

The following are considered offences:

- Manufacturing cosmetics without a valid license.
    
- Sale of misbranded cosmetics.
    
- Sale of spurious cosmetics.
    
- Sale of cosmetics containing prohibited substances.
    
- Import or distribution of non-compliant cosmetics.
    
- Failure to maintain records or follow prescribed standards.
    

---

# 5. Penalties

Persons guilty of violations may face:

### A. Imprisonment

- Depending on the nature and seriousness of the offence.
    

### B. Fine

- Monetary penalties imposed by the court.
    

### C. License Suspension or Cancellation

- Manufacturing license may be suspended or cancelled.
    

### D. Seizure and Confiscation

- Non-compliant cosmetic products may be seized by regulatory authorities.

# Conclusion

The manufacture of cosmetics in India is strictly regulated through licensing, GMP requirements, qualified personnel, and quality control systems. The Drugs and Cosmetics Act prohibits the manufacture and sale of misbranded, spurious, or unsafe cosmetics. Loan licenses provide an alternative manufacturing route, while offences are punishable through fines, imprisonment, product seizure, and cancellation of licenses.
