# Structure of Skin Relating to Dry Skin, Acne, Pigmentation, Prickly Heat, Wrinkles and Body Odor (10 Marks)

## Introduction

The skin is the largest organ of the body and acts as a protective barrier. It consists of three main layers:

1. **Epidermis**
    
2. **Dermis**
    
3. **Hypodermis (Subcutaneous tissue)**
    

Many cosmetic problems arise due to structural or functional abnormalities in specific parts of the skin.

---

# Structure of Skin

## 1. Epidermis

Outermost layer of skin.

### Layers:

- Stratum corneum
    
- Stratum lucidum (thick skin only)
    
- Stratum granulosum
    
- Stratum spinosum
    
- Stratum basale
    

### Functions:

- Protection
    
- Prevents water loss
    
- Melanin production by melanocytes
    

---

## 2. Dermis

Middle layer of skin.

Contains:

- Collagen fibers
    
- Elastin fibers
    
- Blood vessels
    
- Nerves
    
- Hair follicles
    
- Sebaceous glands
    
- Sweat glands
    

### Functions:

- Strength
    
- Elasticity
    
- Nutrition to epidermis
    

---

## 3. Hypodermis

Innermost layer.

Contains:

- Adipose tissue (fat)
    
- Connective tissue
    

### Functions:

- Insulation
    
- Shock absorption
    
- Energy storage
    

---

# Skin Structure Related to Common Problems

## 1. Dry Skin (Xerosis)

### Structural Site

**Stratum Corneum**

### Cause

- Loss of Natural Moisturizing Factor (NMF)
    
- Decreased ceramides
    
- Increased transepidermal water loss (TEWL)
    

### Features

- Roughness
    
- Scaling
    
- Cracking
    
- Itching
    

**Key Point:** Dry skin occurs due to failure of the epidermal barrier.

---

## 2. Acne

### Structural Site

**Pilosebaceous Unit**  
(Hair follicle + Sebaceous gland)

### Cause

- Excess sebum production
    
- Follicular blockage
    
- Growth of _Cutibacterium acnes_
    
- Inflammation
    

### Features

- Comedones
    
- Papules
    
- Pustules
    

**Key Point:** Acne is a disorder of the pilosebaceous unit.

---

## 3. Pigmentation

### Structural Site

**Melanocytes in Stratum Basale**

### Cause

- Increased melanin synthesis
    
- Increased tyrosinase activity
    
- UV exposure
    
- Hormonal changes
    

### Features

- Melasma
    
- Age spots
    
- Hyperpigmentation
    

**Key Point:** Pigmentation results from melanocyte dysfunction.

---

## 4. Prickly Heat (Miliaria)

### Structural Site

**Eccrine Sweat Glands and Ducts**

### Cause

- Blockage of sweat ducts
    
- Sweat retention
    
- Hot and humid climate
    

### Features

- Red rashes
    
- Pricking sensation
    
- Burning and itching
    

**Key Point:** Prickly heat occurs due to obstruction of eccrine sweat ducts.

---

## 5. Wrinkles

### Structural Site

**Dermis**

### Cause

- Decrease in collagen
    
- Decrease in elastin
    
- Hyaluronic acid depletion
    
- UV-induced aging
    

### Features

- Fine lines
    
- Skin sagging
    
- Loss of elasticity
    

**Key Point:** Wrinkles arise from degeneration of dermal collagen and elastin fibers.

---

## 6. Body Odor (Bromhidrosis)

### Structural Site

**Apocrine Sweat Glands**

### Cause

- Apocrine sweat secretion
    
- Bacterial decomposition by skin flora
    
- Production of odoriferous compounds
    

### Features

- Unpleasant odor, especially in axillae and groin
    

**Key Point:** Body odor results from bacterial breakdown of apocrine gland secretions.

---

# Summary Table (Very Important)

|Condition|Main Structural Site|Main Cause|
|---|---|---|
|Dry Skin|Stratum Corneum|Loss of water and lipids|
|Acne|Pilosebaceous Unit|Sebum + bacteria|
|Pigmentation|Melanocytes (Basal Layer)|Excess melanin|
|Prickly Heat|Eccrine Sweat Duct|Sweat duct blockage|
|Wrinkles|Dermis|Collagen & elastin loss|
|Body Odor|Apocrine Gland|Bacterial decomposition|

---

# Conclusion

The skin consists of **epidermis, dermis, and hypodermis**. Each cosmetic problem is associated with a specific skin structure: **dry skin with stratum corneum, acne with pilosebaceous units, pigmentation with melanocytes, prickly heat with eccrine ducts, wrinkles with dermal collagen and elastin, and body odor with apocrine glands**. Understanding these relationships helps in the development of effective cosmetic and cosmeceutical products.
