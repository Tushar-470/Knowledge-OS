# Indian Regulatory Requirements for Labeling of Cosmetics (10 Marks)

## Introduction

Labeling of cosmetics in India is regulated under the **Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940**, **Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945 (Rule 145)**, **Cosmetics Rules, 2020**, and **Legal Metrology (Packaged Commodities) Rules, 2011**. Labeling provides essential information regarding identity, safety, quality, and traceability of cosmetic products.

---

# Legal Framework

|Regulation|Purpose|
|---|---|
|Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940|Prevents misbranding and ensures safety|
|Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945 (Rule 145)|Prescribes labeling requirements|
|Cosmetics Rules, 2020|Updated cosmetic regulations|
|Legal Metrology Rules, 2011|Net quantity, MRP, manufacturer details|

---

# Mandatory Labeling Requirements (Rule 145)

## 1. Name of the Cosmetic

- The label must clearly state the name of the cosmetic product.
    
- The name should not mislead consumers regarding its purpose.
    

**Example:** Moisturizing Cream, Shampoo, Lipstick.

---

## 2. Name and Address of Manufacturer

- Full name and complete address of the manufacturer must be mentioned.
    
- Helps in product traceability and consumer complaints.
    

---

## 3. Net Contents

- Quantity of product must be declared.
    
- Expressed in:
    
    - Weight (g, kg) for solids
        
    - Volume (mL, L) for liquids
        
    - Number for unit items
        

Example: Net Volume 100 mL.

---

## 4. Batch Number

- Every cosmetic must bear a **batch number** preceded by:
    

**“B”**

- Used for identification and recall of defective batches.
    

---

## 5. Manufacturing License Number

- Manufacturing license number must be displayed.
    
- Usually preceded by:
    

**“M”**

---

## 6. List of Ingredients

- Ingredients must be listed in descending order of concentration.
    
- Ingredients present in concentration less than 1% may be listed in any order.
    

---

## 7. Directions for Safe Use

- Necessary instructions for use should be provided.
    
- Particularly important for hair dyes, depilatories, and other products with special precautions.
    

---

## 8. Warning Statements

Where applicable, cautionary statements must be mentioned.

### Examples:

- “For external use only”
    
- “Avoid contact with eyes”
    
- “Keep out of reach of children”
    

---

## 9. Declaration for Imported Cosmetics

Imported cosmetics must additionally mention:

- Name and address of importer
    
- Country of origin
    

This ensures accountability of imported products.

---

## 10. MRP and Consumer Information

As per Legal Metrology Rules:

- Maximum Retail Price (MRP)
    
- Net quantity
    
- Consumer information
    

must be displayed clearly.

---

# Cosmetics Exempted from Ingredient Declaration

Very small packs are exempted from full ingredient declaration:

- Solid or semi-solid cosmetics ≤ 10 g
    
- Liquid cosmetics ≤ 25 mL
    

(Important university point)

---

# Misbranding Related to Labeling

A cosmetic is considered **misbranded** if:

- Label contains false or misleading claims.
    
- Mandatory information is absent.
    
- Unauthorized colors are used.
    
- Packaging deceives consumers.
    

---

# Conclusion

The labeling requirements under **Rule 145 of the Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945** ensure proper identification, safe use, quality assurance, and traceability of cosmetics. A compliant label must contain product name, manufacturer details, batch number, license number, ingredient list, net contents, warnings, and importer details (for imported products).
