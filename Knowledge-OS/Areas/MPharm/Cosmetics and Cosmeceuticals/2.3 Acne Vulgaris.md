# Acne Vulgaris (10 Marks)

## Introduction

**Acne vulgaris** is a chronic inflammatory disorder of the **pilosebaceous unit** (hair follicle + sebaceous gland). It is one of the most common skin disorders, especially during adolescence, and is characterized by comedones, papules, pustules, nodules, and cysts.

---

# Definition

> **Acne vulgaris is a chronic inflammatory disease of the pilosebaceous unit caused by increased sebum production, follicular hyperkeratinization, bacterial proliferation, and inflammation.**

---

# Structure Involved

## Pilosebaceous Unit

It consists of:

- Hair follicle
    
- Sebaceous gland
    
- Sebum-producing cells
    

The sebaceous gland secretes **sebum**, which lubricates the skin and hair. Acne develops when this unit becomes blocked and inflamed.

---

# Four Main Causes of Acne

## 1. Increased Sebum Production

- Stimulated by androgens during puberty.
    
- Excess sebum accumulates in follicles.
    

## 2. Follicular Hyperkeratinization

- Dead skin cells block the follicular opening.
    
- Formation of **microcomedones**.
    

## 3. Bacterial Proliferation

- Growth of **Cutibacterium acnes (C. acnes)** inside blocked follicles.
    
- Bacteria break down sebum and produce irritants.
    

## 4. Inflammation

- Release of inflammatory mediators causes redness and swelling.

# Types of Acne Lesions

### Non-Inflammatory

- Open comedones (Blackheads)
    
- Closed comedones (Whiteheads)
    

### Inflammatory

- Papules
    
- Pustules
    
- Nodules
    
- Cysts
    

---

# Clinical Features

- Oily skin
    
- Blackheads
    
- Whiteheads
    
- Red papules
    
- Pustules
    
- Nodules
    
- Scarring in severe cases
    

---

# Cosmetic and Cosmeceutical Management

## 1. Salicylic Acid

- Unclogs pores
    
- Reduces keratin buildup
    

## 2. Benzoyl Peroxide

- Kills _C. acnes_
    
- Reduces inflammation
    

## 3. Retinoids

- Normalize follicular keratinization
    
- Prevent comedone formation
    

## 4. Niacinamide

- Reduces sebum production
    
- Anti-inflammatory
    

## 5. Azelaic Acid

- Anti-acne
    
- Reduces post-inflammatory pigmentation
    

---

# Prevention

- Regular cleansing
    
- Avoid oily cosmetics
    
- Maintain skin hygiene
    
- Avoid squeezing pimples
    
- Use non-comedogenic products
    

---

# Conclusion

Acne vulgaris is a disorder of the **pilosebaceous unit** caused by four major factors: **excess sebum production, follicular hyperkeratinization, Cutibacterium acnes proliferation, and inflammation**. Proper skin care and anti-acne agents such as salicylic acid, benzoyl peroxide, retinoids, and niacinamide help control acne effectively.
