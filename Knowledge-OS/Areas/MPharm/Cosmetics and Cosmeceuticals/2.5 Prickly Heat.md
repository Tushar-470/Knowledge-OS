# Prickly Heat (Miliaria) (10 Marks)

## Introduction

**Prickly heat (Miliaria)** is a common skin disorder caused by **blockage of eccrine sweat ducts**, resulting in retention of sweat beneath the skin. It commonly occurs in hot and humid climates and is characterized by itching, prickling, burning sensation, and red rashes.

---

# Definition

> **Prickly heat (Miliaria) is an inflammatory skin condition caused by obstruction of eccrine sweat ducts, leading to sweat retention and development of itchy, prickling eruptions.**

---

# Structure Involved

## Eccrine Sweat Glands

- Distributed throughout the body.
    
- Responsible for thermoregulation.
    
- Sweat is secreted through eccrine ducts onto the skin surface.
    

When the duct becomes blocked, sweat cannot reach the skin surface and accumulates within the skin.

---

# Causes of Prickly Heat

### 1. Hot and Humid Climate

- Excessive sweating causes duct blockage.
    

### 2. Tight Clothing

- Prevents evaporation of sweat.
    

### 3. Poor Ventilation

- Increases sweat accumulation.
    

### 4. Excessive Physical Activity

- Produces large amounts of sweat.
    

### 5. Fever

- Increased sweating may obstruct ducts.
    

---

---

# Types of Miliaria

## 1. Miliaria Crystallina

- Most superficial type.
    
- Small clear vesicles.
    
- No inflammation.
    

## 2. Miliaria Rubra (Prickly Heat)

- Most common form.
    
- Red papules and vesicles.
    
- Intense itching and prickling sensation.
    

## 3. Miliaria Profunda

- Deep obstruction of sweat ducts.
    
- Flesh-colored papules.
    
- May reduce sweating (anhidrosis).
    

---

# Clinical Features

- Small red eruptions
    
- Pricking sensation
    
- Burning sensation
    
- Itching
    
- Excess sweating
    
- Discomfort in hot weather
    

---

# Cosmetic and Cosmeceutical Management

## 1. Cooling the Skin

- Stay in cool environment.
    
- Reduce sweating.
    

## 2. Lightweight Non-Occlusive Products

- Gels preferred over heavy creams.
    

## 3. Calamine Lotion

- Provides cooling effect.
    
- Reduces irritation and itching.
    

## 4. Mild Antimicrobials

- Reduce microbial involvement.
    

## 5. Anti-inflammatory Agents

- Aloe vera
    
- Panthenol
    
- Colloidal oatmeal
    

---

# Prevention

- Wear loose cotton clothing.
    
- Avoid excessive heat exposure.
    
- Maintain proper hygiene.
    
- Keep skin cool and dry.
    
- Avoid heavy oily cosmetics.
    

---

# Conclusion

Prickly heat (Miliaria) is caused by **obstruction of eccrine sweat ducts**, leading to sweat retention and inflammation. It is common in hot and humid conditions and presents with itching, burning, and prickling sensations. Proper cooling, hygiene, and use of non-occlusive products help in prevention and management.
