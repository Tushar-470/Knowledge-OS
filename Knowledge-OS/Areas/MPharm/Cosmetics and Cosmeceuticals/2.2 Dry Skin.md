# Dry Skin (Xerosis) (10 Marks)

## Introduction

**Dry skin (Xerosis)** is a common skin condition characterized by insufficient moisture in the **stratum corneum**, resulting in rough, flaky, dull, and sometimes itchy skin. It occurs due to impairment of the skin barrier and excessive loss of water from the skin surface.

---

# Definition

> **Dry skin (Xerosis) is a condition in which the skin loses its normal moisture content due to decreased hydration of the stratum corneum and increased transepidermal water loss (TEWL).**

---

# Structure Involved

### Stratum Corneum (Outer Epidermal Layer)

The stratum corneum acts as the primary barrier against water loss and contains:

- Corneocytes (dead skin cells)
    
- Intercellular lipids (ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids)
    
- Natural Moisturizing Factor (NMF)
    

Damage to these components causes dry skin.

---

# Causes of Dry Skin

## 1. Ceramide Deficiency

- Reduction in skin lipids disrupts the barrier.
    
- Water escapes easily from the skin.
    

## 2. NMF Deficiency

- Reduced Natural Moisturizing Factor decreases water retention.
    

## 3. Increased TEWL

- Excessive evaporation of water through the skin.
    

## 4. Environmental Factors

- Cold weather
    
- Low humidity
    
- Excessive sun exposure
    
- Frequent washing
    

## 5. Harsh Soaps and Detergents

- Remove natural oils from the skin.
    

---

# Clinical Features

- Rough skin
    
- Dryness
    
- Scaling
    
- Flaking
    
- Tightness
    
- Itching (Pruritus)
    
- Cracks and fissures in severe cases
    

---

# Pathophysiology

```text
Loss of Ceramides/NMF
          ↓
Barrier Damage
          ↓
Increased TEWL
          ↓
Reduced Skin Hydration
          ↓
Dry, Rough and Flaky Skin
```

---

# Management and Cosmetic Care

## 1. Occlusive Agents

Prevent water loss.

Examples:

- Petrolatum
    
- Mineral oil
    
- Dimethicone
    

## 2. Humectants

Attract water into skin.

Examples:

- Glycerin
    
- Urea
    
- Hyaluronic acid
    
- Sodium PCA
    

## 3. Emollients

Fill spaces between skin cells and soften skin.

Examples:

- Fatty alcohols
    
- Esters
    
- Squalane
    

## 4. Ceramide-Based Moisturizers

Restore the damaged skin barrier.

---

# Prevention

- Use mild cleansers.
    
- Avoid hot water bathing.
    
- Apply moisturizer regularly.
    
- Protect skin from cold and dry weather.
    
- Maintain adequate hydration.
    

---

# Conclusion

Dry skin (xerosis) results from **barrier dysfunction of the stratum corneum**, leading to increased transepidermal water loss and reduced skin hydration. Deficiency of ceramides and NMF are major causes. Proper use of moisturizers containing **humectants, emollients, and occlusives** helps restore skin hydration and barrier function.
