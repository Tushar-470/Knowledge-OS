# Feet: Cleansing and Care Needs (10 Marks)

## Introduction

The feet bear the entire body weight and are constantly exposed to **pressure, friction, sweat, dust, and microorganisms**. The skin of the soles is thick and lacks sebaceous glands, making it prone to dryness, calluses, cracks, fungal infections, and foot odor. Therefore, proper cleansing and care are essential for healthy feet.

---

# Characteristics of Foot Skin

- Thick stratum corneum, especially on soles.
    
- No sebaceous glands on soles.
    
- High number of sweat glands.
    
- Subjected to continuous pressure and friction.
    
- Prone to calluses, corns, fissures, and fungal infections.
    

---

# Cleansing Needs of Feet

## 1. Removal of Dirt and Sweat

- Daily washing removes dirt, sweat, and microorganisms.
    
- Helps prevent infections and bad odor.
    

## 2. Prevention of Microbial Growth

- Warm and moist conditions inside footwear favor fungal and bacterial growth.
    
- Proper cleansing reduces infection risk.
    

## 3. Proper Drying

- Feet should be dried thoroughly, especially between toes.
    
- Prevents fungal infections such as athlete's foot.
    

### Recommended Cleansers

- Mild soap
    
- Antimicrobial foot cleansers
    
- Foot washes containing antiseptic agents
    

---

# Care Needs of Feet

## 1. Moisturization

Since soles lack sebaceous glands, they dry easily.

### Benefits

- Prevents roughness and cracking.
    
- Maintains skin softness.
    

### Ingredients

- Urea
    
- Glycerin
    
- Lactic acid
    
- Ceramides
    

---

## 2. Prevention of Calluses and Corns

### Causes

- Continuous pressure and friction.
    

### Care

- Regular exfoliation.
    
- Keratolytic preparations.
    

### Ingredients

- Salicylic acid
    
- Urea (20–40%)
    

---

## 3. Prevention of Cracked Heels (Fissures)

### Causes

- Severe dryness
    
- Prolonged standing
    
- Improper footwear
    

### Care

- Regular moisturization.
    
- Use of heel creams.
    

---

## 4. Control of Foot Odor

### Cause

- Bacterial decomposition of sweat.
    

### Care

- Foot powders
    
- Antiperspirants
    
- Proper hygiene
    

### Ingredients

- Zinc oxide
    
- Talc
    
- Aluminum salts
    

---

## 5. Prevention of Fungal Infection

### Common Infection

- Athlete's foot (Tinea pedis)
    

### Care

- Keep feet dry.
    
- Wear clean socks.
    
- Use antifungal powders if necessary.
    

---

# Common Problems of Feet

|Problem|Cause|
|---|---|
|Dryness|Lack of sebaceous glands|
|Calluses|Pressure and friction|
|Corns|Repeated pressure|
|Cracked heels|Severe dryness|
|Foot odor|Bacterial action on sweat|
|Fungal infections|Moist environment|

---

# Conclusion

Foot care involves **daily cleansing, proper drying, moisturization, odor control, and prevention of calluses, cracks, and fungal infections**. Regular use of moisturizers containing **urea and glycerin**, along with proper hygiene, helps maintain healthy feet.
