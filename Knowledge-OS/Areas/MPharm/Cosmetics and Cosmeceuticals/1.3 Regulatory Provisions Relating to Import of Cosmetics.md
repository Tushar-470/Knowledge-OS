# Regulatory Provisions Relating to Import of Cosmetics (10 Marks)

## Introduction

The import of cosmetics into India is regulated to ensure that only safe, quality, and legally compliant cosmetic products enter the Indian market. The regulatory framework is governed by the **Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940**, **Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945**, **Cosmetics Rules, 2020**, and enforced by the **Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation (CDSCO)**.

---

# Legal Provisions Governing Import of Cosmetics

|Provision|Purpose|
|---|---|
|Section 10A|Power to prohibit import of unsafe cosmetics|
|Section 11|Import allowed only under a valid license|
|Section 12|Appointment of Cosmetics Import Officers|
|Cosmetics Rules, 2020|Import procedures and compliance requirements|
|Customs Act, 1962|Clearance and inspection at ports|
|Foreign Trade Policy|Import Export Code (IEC) requirements|

---

# 1. Import License Requirement

- Any person or company importing cosmetics for commercial purposes must obtain an **Import License** from **CDSCO**.
    
- The license is issued by the **Central Licensing Authority (CLA)**.
    
- Import license is different from a manufacturing license.
    

### Important Point

**Import License ≠ Manufacturing License**

A manufacturer cannot import cosmetics without a separate import license and vice versa.

---

# 2. Application for Import License

The application is submitted in **Form COS-8** under the Cosmetics Rules, 2020.

### Documents Required

1. Business registration details
    
2. Import Export Code (IEC)
    
3. GST registration
    
4. Storage premises details
    
5. Declaration of compliance with Indian regulations
    
6. Prescribed fee payment
    

---

# 3. Labeling Requirements for Imported Cosmetics

Imported cosmetics must comply with Indian labeling requirements and bear:

- Name of cosmetic
    
- Manufacturer's name and address
    
- Importer's name and address
    
- Country of origin
    
- Batch number
    
- Manufacturing license details
    
- Net contents
    
- Ingredient list
    

If required information is absent, a supplementary sticker label may be affixed before sale in India.

---

# 4. Ingredient Compliance

- Imported cosmetics must comply with the ingredient requirements specified under the **Cosmetics Rules, 2020**.
    
- Cosmetics containing prohibited substances are not permitted for import even if allowed in the country of manufacture.
    

---

# 5. Inspection and Testing at Ports

Under **Section 12**, Cosmetics Import Officers may:

- Inspect consignments
    
- Collect samples
    
- Send samples for laboratory testing
    
- Detain or seize non-compliant products
    

---

# 6. Prohibition of Import

The Central Government may prohibit import of cosmetics that are:

- Unsafe
    
- Harmful to health
    
- Misbranded
    
- Spurious
    
- Not of standard quality
    

as provided under **Section 10A of the Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940**.

---

# 7. Exemptions from Import License

Under **Rule 43A**, small quantities of cosmetics imported for:

- Personal use
    
- Gift purposes
    
- Trade samples
    
- Examination and testing
    

may be exempted from full licensing requirements.

---

# Flow Chart of Cosmetic Import

```text
Foreign Manufacturer
          ↓
Import License (COS-8)
          ↓
CDSCO Approval
          ↓
Customs & Port Inspection
          ↓
Labeling Compliance Check
          ↓
Quality & Ingredient Verification
          ↓
Distribution in Indian Market
```

---

# Conclusion

The import of cosmetics into India is regulated through the **Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940**, **Cosmetics Rules, 2020**, and **CDSCO**. Importers must obtain a valid import license, comply with labeling and ingredient requirements, and undergo inspection by Customs and CDSCO authorities before products can be marketed in India.

## Examiner's Bonus Point ⭐

**Imported cosmetics must comply with Indian standards even if they are legally marketed in the country of origin.** This is a commonly asked viva and theory point.
