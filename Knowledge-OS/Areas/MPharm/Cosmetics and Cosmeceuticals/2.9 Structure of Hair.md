# Structure of Hair (10 Marks)

## Introduction

Hair is a keratinized filamentous structure arising from the **hair follicle** present in the skin. It is composed of a visible part called the **hair shaft** and an embedded part called the **hair root/follicle**. Hair provides protection, insulation, and enhances appearance.

---

# Structure of Hair

Hair consists of two major parts:

## 1. Hair Shaft

The hair shaft is the visible portion above the skin surface. It is made up of dead, keratinized cells and has three concentric layers.

### A. Cuticle

- Outermost layer.
    
- Formed by overlapping scale-like cells.
    
- Protects inner layers from physical and chemical damage.
    
- Responsible for hair smoothness and shine.
    

### B. Cortex

- Middle and thickest layer (80–90% of hair).
    
- Contains keratin fibers and melanin pigment.
    
- Provides strength, elasticity, and hair color.
    

### C. Medulla

- Innermost central core.
    
- Contains loosely packed cells and air spaces.
    
- More prominent in thick hair.

## 2. Hair Follicle (Hair Root)

The hair follicle is the portion present inside the skin and is responsible for hair growth.

### Parts of Hair Follicle

### A. Hair Bulb

- Enlarged base of hair follicle.
    
- Site of active hair formation.
    

### B. Dermal Papilla

- Located at the base of the bulb.
    
- Contains blood vessels and nerves.
    
- Supplies nutrients to growing hair.
    

### C. Hair Matrix

- Surrounds the dermal papilla.
    
- Rapidly dividing cells produce new hair.
    

### D. Melanocytes

- Present in hair bulb.
    
- Produce melanin pigment responsible for hair color.
    

### E. Sebaceous Gland

- Opens into hair follicle.
    
- Secretes sebum that lubricates hair and scalp.
    

### F. Arrector Pili Muscle

- Smooth muscle attached to follicle.
    
- Causes erection of hair ("goose bumps").

# Functions of Hair

- Protection from UV radiation and injury.
    
- Thermal insulation.
    
- Sensory function.
    
- Improves appearance and aesthetics.

# Conclusion

Hair consists of a **hair shaft (cuticle, cortex, medulla)** and a **hair follicle (hair bulb, matrix, dermal papilla, sebaceous gland, and melanocytes)**. The coordinated function of these structures is responsible for hair growth, strength, texture, and color.
