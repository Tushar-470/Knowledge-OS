# Pigmentation (Hyperpigmentation) (10 Marks)

## Introduction

**Pigmentation** refers to the color of the skin produced by **melanin**, a pigment synthesized by **melanocytes** present in the **stratum basale of the epidermis**. Hyperpigmentation occurs when excess melanin is produced or unevenly distributed in the skin, leading to dark patches or spots.

---

# Definition

> **Pigmentation is a condition characterized by increased melanin production or abnormal melanin distribution, resulting in darkening of the skin.**

---

# Structure Involved

## Melanocytes (Stratum Basale)

- Located in the basal layer of the epidermis.
    
- Produce melanin within organelles called **melanosomes**.
    
- One melanocyte supplies pigment to many surrounding keratinocytes.
    

### Melanin Formation

```text
Tyrosine
   ↓ (Tyrosinase)
DOPA
   ↓
Dopaquinone
   ↓
Melanin
```

**Tyrosinase** is the key enzyme controlling melanin synthesis.

---

# Causes of Pigmentation

## 1. UV Radiation

- Stimulates melanocytes.
    
- Increases melanin production.
    

## 2. Hormonal Changes

- Pregnancy
    
- Oral contraceptives
    
- Melasma
    

## 3. Inflammation

- Acne
    
- Eczema
    
- Skin injury
    

## 4. Aging

- Development of age spots (solar lentigines).
    

## 5. Certain Drugs and Cosmetics

- May stimulate melanogenesis.
    

---

# Types of Pigmentation

## 1. Melasma

- Brown patches on face.
    
- Associated with hormonal changes and sun exposure.
    

## 2. Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation (PIH)

- Occurs after acne, burns, eczema, or injury.
    

## 3. Solar Lentigines (Age Spots)

- Caused by long-term sun exposure.
    

## 4. Periorbital Hyperpigmentation

- Dark circles around the eyes.
    

---

# Pathophysiology

```text
UV Exposure / Hormones / Inflammation
                ↓
      Activation of Melanocytes
                ↓
      Increased Tyrosinase Activity
                ↓
         Increased Melanin
                ↓
          Hyperpigmentation
```

---

# Clinical Features

- Dark patches on skin
    
- Uneven skin tone
    
- Brown or black spots
    
- Facial discoloration
    
- Dark circles around eyes
    

---

# Cosmetic and Cosmeceutical Management

## 1. Tyrosinase Inhibitors

Reduce melanin synthesis.

Examples:

- Kojic acid
    
- Arbutin
    
- Tranexamic acid
    

## 2. Niacinamide

- Inhibits melanosome transfer.
    
- Brightens skin tone.
    

## 3. Retinoids

- Increase epidermal turnover.
    
- Help remove pigmented cells.
    

## 4. Sunscreens (Most Important)

- Prevent UV-induced melanogenesis.
    
- Broad-spectrum SPF is essential.
    

## 5. Vitamin C

- Antioxidant
    
- Reduces melanin formation
    

---

# Prevention

- Daily use of sunscreen (SPF ≥ 30)
    
- Avoid excessive sun exposure
    
- Wear protective clothing
    
- Early treatment of acne and inflammation
    

---

# Conclusion

Pigmentation is mainly caused by **overactivity of melanocytes in the stratum basale**, leading to excess melanin production. Major causes include **UV exposure, hormonal changes, inflammation, and aging**. Management focuses on **tyrosinase inhibition, increased cell turnover, and sun protection**.
