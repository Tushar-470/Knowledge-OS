# Misbranded and Spurious Cosmetics (10 Marks)

## Introduction

To protect consumers from unsafe and deceptive cosmetic products, the **Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940** classifies certain cosmetics as **Misbranded** and **Spurious**. Such products are prohibited from manufacture, sale, distribution, and import in India.

---

# 1. Misbranded Cosmetics

## Definition

According to **Section 14 of the Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940**, a cosmetic is considered **misbranded** when its labeling, packaging, or presentation is false, misleading, or does not comply with legal requirements.

### Conditions Under Which a Cosmetic is Misbranded

### 1. Contains Unauthorized Colors

- If it contains a color other than those permitted under the Cosmetics Rules.
    
- Use of non-approved color additives makes the cosmetic misbranded.
    

### 2. Misleading Packaging

- If the container is designed or filled in a way that deceives consumers.
    
- Example: Large container containing very little product (slack fill).
    

### 3. False or Misleading Claims

- Claims benefits that the product does not possess.
    
- Example: A fairness cream claiming "permanent skin whitening in 7 days."
    

### 4. Improper Labeling

- Absence of mandatory information such as:
    
    - Manufacturer's name
        
    - Batch number
        
    - Ingredients
        
    - Net contents
        
    - Warnings
        

makes the cosmetic misbranded.

---

## Examples of Misbranded Cosmetics

- Shampoo with false anti-hair loss claims.
    
- Cream containing unapproved color additives.
    
- Product with incorrect ingredient declaration.
    
- Cosmetic sold in deceptive packaging.
    

---

# 2. Spurious Cosmetics

## Definition

A cosmetic is considered **spurious** when it is falsely represented as another product or manufacturer with the intention of deceiving consumers. It is essentially a counterfeit cosmetic.

### Conditions Under Which a Cosmetic is Spurious

### 1. Imitation of Another Cosmetic

- Product imitates or substitutes another genuine cosmetic.
    

### 2. False Manufacturer Name

- Label bears the name of a manufacturer who did not actually produce it.
    

### 3. Counterfeit Product

- Product is sold under the name of a famous brand without authorization.
    

### 4. False Identity

- Product is made to appear as another established cosmetic brand.
    

---

## Examples of Spurious Cosmetics

- Fake Lakmé lipstick sold as original.
    
- Counterfeit shampoo using another company's trademark.
    
- Duplicate perfume marketed under a reputed brand name.
    
- Cosmetic bearing a false manufacturer's address.
    

---

# Difference Between Misbranded and Spurious Cosmetics

|Misbranded Cosmetic|Spurious Cosmetic|
|---|---|
|Misleading labeling or packaging|Fake or counterfeit product|
|Product may be genuine|Product identity is false|
|Violates labeling requirements|Violates identity/authenticity|
|Example: False claim on label|Example: Duplicate branded lipstick|

---

# Regulatory Action

Manufacture, sale, distribution, or import of misbranded and spurious cosmetics is prohibited under the **Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940**. Regulatory authorities may:

- Seize products
    
- Cancel licenses
    
- Prosecute offenders
    
- Impose penalties and imprisonment
    

---

# Conclusion

**Misbranded cosmetics** mislead consumers through false labeling, packaging, or claims, whereas **spurious cosmetics** are counterfeit products that imitate genuine brands. Both are prohibited under the Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 to ensure consumer safety and product authenticity.
