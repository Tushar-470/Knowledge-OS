# Structure of Hair and Hair Growth Cycle (10 Marks)

## Introduction

Hair is a keratinized filament arising from the **hair follicle** present in the skin. It protects the scalp, helps in thermoregulation, and contributes to appearance. Hair growth occurs in a cyclic manner through distinct phases: **Anagen, Catagen, Telogen, and Exogen**.

---

# Structure of Hair

Hair consists of two main parts:

## 1. Hair Shaft

The visible portion above the skin surface.

### Layers of Hair Shaft

### A. Cuticle

- Outermost protective layer.
    
- Made of overlapping keratinized cells.
    
- Provides shine and protection.
    
- Damage causes rough and dull hair.
    

### B. Cortex

- Middle and largest layer (80–90% of hair).
    
- Contains keratin fibers and melanin pigment.
    
- Responsible for strength, elasticity, and hair color.
    

### C. Medulla

- Innermost core.
    
- Contains loosely packed cells.
    
- Present mainly in thick hairs.
    

---

## 2. Hair Follicle

The portion embedded in the skin.

### Important Parts

- **Hair Bulb** – enlarged base of follicle.
    
- **Dermal Papilla** – supplies nutrients and controls growth.
    
- **Hair Matrix** – actively dividing cells producing hair.
    
- **Sebaceous Gland** – secretes sebum.
    
- **Arrector Pili Muscle** – causes hair to stand upright.
    
- **Melanocytes** – produce melanin pigment.

# Hair Growth Cycle

Hair growth occurs in four phases. Each follicle grows independently (asynchronous growth cycle).

## 1. Anagen Phase (Growth Phase)

- Active growth phase.
    
- Hair matrix cells divide rapidly.
    
- Hair length increases continuously.
    
- Lasts **2–7 years**.
    
- About **85–90%** of scalp hairs are in this phase.
    

### Importance

Determines maximum hair length.

---

## 2. Catagen Phase (Regression Phase)

- Short transitional phase.
    
- Hair growth stops.
    
- Lower follicle shrinks.
    
- Hair bulb regresses.
    

### Duration

- About **2–3 weeks**.
    
- Only **1–2%** of hairs.
    

---

## 3. Telogen Phase (Resting Phase)

- Follicle remains inactive.
    
- Club hair remains attached.
    
- No active growth occurs.
    

### Duration

- Approximately **3 months**.
    
- About **10–15%** of scalp hairs.
    

---

## 4. Exogen Phase (Shedding Phase)

- Old hair is shed.
    
- New anagen hair begins to grow.
    
- Normal loss of **50–100 hairs/day** may occur.
    

---

# Conclusion

Hair consists of a **hair shaft (cuticle, cortex, medulla)** and a **hair follicle (hair bulb, matrix, dermal papilla, sebaceous gland)**. Hair growth occurs through the cyclical phases of **anagen, catagen, telogen, and exogen**, ensuring continuous renewal and replacement of hair.
