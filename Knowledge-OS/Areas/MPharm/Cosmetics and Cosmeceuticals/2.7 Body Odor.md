# Body Odor (Bromhidrosis) (10 Marks)

## Introduction

**Body odor (Bromhidrosis)** is an unpleasant smell produced when sweat secreted by **apocrine sweat glands** is broken down by skin microorganisms. The sweat itself is initially odorless, but bacterial action converts it into volatile odor-producing compounds.

---

# Definition

> **Body odor (Bromhidrosis) is the unpleasant odor resulting from bacterial decomposition of apocrine gland secretions on the skin surface.**

---

# Structure Involved

## Apocrine Sweat Glands

### Location

- Axilla (armpits)
    
- Groin
    
- Perianal region
    
- Areola of breast
    

### Characteristics

- Become active after puberty.
    
- Secrete a thick, protein-rich fluid.
    
- Sweat is initially odorless.
    

---

# Mechanism of Body Odor Formation

### Step 1: Apocrine Secretion

- Apocrine glands release protein- and lipid-rich sweat.
    

### Step 2: Bacterial Action

Skin bacteria such as:

- **Corynebacterium spp.**
    
- **Staphylococcus spp.**
    

break down sweat components.

### Step 3: Formation of Odorous Compounds

- Volatile fatty acids
    
- Sulfur-containing compounds
    
- Other odoriferous molecules
    

are produced.

### Step 4: Unpleasant Body Odor

---

# Pathophysiology

```text
Apocrine Sweat Secretion
            ↓
Bacterial Decomposition
(Corynebacterium & Staphylococcus)
            ↓
Production of Volatile Odorous Compounds
            ↓
Body Odor (Bromhidrosis)
```

---

# Factors Increasing Body Odor

## 1. Excessive Sweating

Provides more substrate for bacteria.

## 2. Poor Personal Hygiene

Allows bacterial growth.

## 3. Hot and Humid Climate

Increases sweating.

## 4. Hormonal Changes

Common during puberty.

## 5. Certain Foods

- Garlic
    
- Onion
    
- Spicy foods
    

may worsen body odor.

---

# Clinical Features

- Unpleasant smell
    
- Excessive sweating
    
- More common in axilla and groin
    
- Social embarrassment
    

---

# Cosmetic Management

## 1. Deodorants

### Mechanism

- Reduce bacterial growth.
    
- Mask unpleasant odor with fragrance.
    

### Examples

- Alcohol
    
- Zinc ricinoleate
    
- Antimicrobial agents
    

---

## 2. Antiperspirants

### Mechanism

- Reduce sweat production.
    

### Example

- Aluminum chlorohydrate
    

These form temporary plugs in sweat ducts and decrease sweating.

---

## 3. Good Hygiene

- Regular bathing
    
- Clean clothing
    
- Hair removal in axilla
    

---

# Difference Between Deodorants and Antiperspirants

|Deodorants|Antiperspirants|
|---|---|
|Control odor|Control sweating|
|Act on bacteria|Act on sweat glands|
|Fragrance + antimicrobial action|Aluminum salts used|
|Do not reduce sweat|Reduce sweat production|

---

# Prevention

- Maintain personal hygiene.
    
- Use deodorants or antiperspirants.
    
- Wear clean cotton clothes.
    
- Keep axillary region dry.
    
- Avoid foods that worsen body odor.
    

---

# Conclusion

Body odor (Bromhidrosis) results from the interaction between **apocrine sweat gland secretions and skin bacteria**, particularly **Corynebacterium** and **Staphylococcus** species. The condition can be effectively managed by good hygiene, deodorants, and antiperspirants.
