# Common Problems Associated with Oral Cavity (10 Marks)

## Introduction

The oral cavity is continuously exposed to food, microorganisms, and environmental factors. As a result, several oral problems can occur affecting the teeth, gums, tongue, and supporting tissues. The most common oral problems are **dental caries, gingivitis, periodontitis, halitosis, dentine hypersensitivity, and tooth staining/whitening issues.**

---

# 1. Dental Caries (Tooth Decay)

### Definition

Dental caries is the destruction of tooth enamel due to acids produced by bacteria from dietary sugars.

### Causes

- Poor oral hygiene
    
- Excess sugar intake
    
- Plaque accumulation
    
- Bacterial growth (_Streptococcus mutans_)
    

### Features

- Tooth decay
    
- Pain
    
- Cavities
    
- Tooth sensitivity
    

### Prevention

- Fluoride toothpaste
    
- Proper brushing
    
- Reduced sugar consumption
    

---

# 2. Gingivitis

### Definition

Inflammation of the gums without loss of supporting bone.

### Causes

- Dental plaque
    
- Poor oral hygiene
    

### Features

- Red gums
    
- Swollen gums
    
- Bleeding during brushing
    

### Prevention

- Regular brushing
    
- Mouthwash use
    
- Professional cleaning
    

---

# 3. Periodontitis

### Definition

Advanced gum disease involving destruction of periodontal ligament and alveolar bone.

### Causes

- Untreated gingivitis
    
- Chronic plaque accumulation
    

### Features

- Gum recession
    
- Deep periodontal pockets
    
- Tooth mobility
    
- Tooth loss
    

### Prevention

- Early treatment of gingivitis
    
- Regular dental check-ups
    

---

# 4. Halitosis (Bad Breath)

### Definition

Unpleasant odor from the mouth caused by bacterial breakdown of proteins.

### Causes

- Poor oral hygiene
    
- Tongue coating
    
- Periodontal disease
    
- Food debris
    

### Features

- Bad breath
    
- Unpleasant taste
    

### Prevention

- Tongue cleaning
    
- Mouthwash
    
- Good oral hygiene
    

---

# 5. Dentine Hypersensitivity

### Definition

Sharp pain arising from exposed dentine in response to stimuli.

### Causes

- Enamel erosion
    
- Gingival recession
    
- Excessive brushing
    

### Features

- Pain on cold, hot, sweet, or acidic foods
    

### Management

- Desensitizing toothpaste
    
- Fluoride treatment
    

---

# 6. Tooth Staining

### Types

### A. Extrinsic Staining

Occurs on the tooth surface.

**Causes:**

- Tea
    
- Coffee
    
- Tobacco
    

### B. Intrinsic Staining

Occurs within enamel or dentine.

**Causes:**

- Aging
    
- Drugs
    
- Trauma
    

### Management

- Whitening toothpaste
    
- Professional bleaching
    

---

---

# Conclusion

The major oral cavity problems include **dental caries, gingivitis, periodontitis, halitosis, dentine hypersensitivity, and tooth staining**. Most of these conditions are associated with poor oral hygiene and plaque accumulation and can be prevented through proper oral care practices.
