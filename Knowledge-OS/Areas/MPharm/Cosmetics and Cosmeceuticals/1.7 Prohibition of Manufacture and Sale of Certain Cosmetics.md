# Prohibition of Manufacture and Sale of Certain Cosmetics (10 Marks)

## Introduction

To protect public health and ensure consumer safety, the **Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940** prohibits the manufacture and sale of certain cosmetics that are unsafe, misleading, counterfeit, or not of standard quality. No person shall manufacture, sell, stock, distribute, or offer such cosmetics for sale in India.

---

# Prohibition of Manufacture and Sale of Certain Cosmetics

Under the Drugs and Cosmetics Act, the following cosmetics are prohibited:

## 1. Misbranded Cosmetics

A cosmetic is prohibited if it is **misbranded**, i.e.,

- Contains unauthorized colours.
    
- Has false or misleading labeling.
    
- Is packed in a deceptive manner.
    
- Makes false claims regarding its effects.
    

**Example:** Fairness cream claiming permanent skin whitening.

---

## 2. Spurious Cosmetics

A cosmetic is prohibited if it is **spurious**, meaning:

- It imitates another cosmetic.
    
- Bears the name of a manufacturer who did not manufacture it.
    
- Is a counterfeit product.
    
- Is sold under a false brand identity.
    

**Example:** Duplicate branded lipstick or perfume sold as original.

---

## 3. Cosmetics Not of Standard Quality

Manufacture or sale is prohibited if the cosmetic:

- Does not comply with prescribed standards.
    
- Fails quality, safety, or purity requirements.
    
- Contains harmful contaminants.
    

---

## 4. Cosmetics Containing Prohibited Ingredients

No cosmetic shall be manufactured or sold if it contains:

- Banned substances.
    
- Harmful chemicals beyond permitted limits.
    
- Ingredients prohibited under Cosmetics Rules, 2020.
    

---

## 5. Cosmetics Manufactured Without License

Any cosmetic manufactured without obtaining a valid manufacturing license from the licensing authority is prohibited.

---

## 6. Cosmetics Imported in Violation of Rules

Cosmetics imported without complying with:

- Import license requirements
    
- Labeling requirements
    
- Ingredient requirements
    

cannot be legally sold in India.

---

# Consequences of Violation

Regulatory authorities may:

- Seize products
    
- Stop sale and distribution
    
- Suspend or cancel licenses
    
- Prosecute offenders
    
- Impose fines and imprisonment

# Conclusion

The Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 prohibits the manufacture and sale of **misbranded, spurious, substandard, unlicensed, and unsafe cosmetics**. These provisions protect consumers from harmful products and ensure that only safe and quality cosmetics are available in the market.
