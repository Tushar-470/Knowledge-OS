# ASBT (Apical Sodium-Dependent Bile Acid Transporter) (10 Marks)

## Introduction

**ASBT (Apical Sodium-Dependent Bile Acid Transporter)** is an important influx transporter responsible for the absorption and recycling of bile acids from the intestine. It plays a crucial role in **enterohepatic circulation**, maintaining the body's bile acid pool and influencing the absorption of lipids, fat-soluble vitamins, and certain drugs. ASBT is also an important target in drug development and computational drug disposition studies.

---

# Definition

**ASBT** is a sodium-dependent membrane transporter encoded by the **SLC10A2 gene** that actively transports bile acids from the intestinal lumen into enterocytes.

# Location of ASBT

ASBT is primarily present in:

- Terminal Ileum (major site)
    
- Small Intestine
    
- Bile Duct Epithelium (low levels)
    

### Major Site

**Terminal Ileum**

About **95% of bile acids** are reabsorbed here.

---

# Mechanism of Transport

ASBT utilizes the sodium gradient to transport bile acids into intestinal cells.

### Step 1

Bile acids present in intestinal lumen.

### Step 2

Na⁺ binds to ASBT.

### Step 3

Bile acid binds to transporter.

### Step 4

Both enter the intestinal epithelial cell.

```text
Intestinal Lumen
        ↓
 Bile Acid + Na+
        ↓
       ASBT
        ↓
    Enterocyte
        ↓
 Portal Circulation
        ↓
      Liver
```

### Importance

- Conserves bile acids.
    
- Maintains digestion of fats.
    
- Reduces need for new bile acid synthesis.

# Clinical Importance

## 1. Lipid Absorption

Bile acids are essential for:

- Fat digestion
    
- Cholesterol absorption
    
- Fat-soluble vitamin absorption
    

(Vitamins A, D, E, K)

---

## 2. Drug Delivery

ASBT can be targeted to improve intestinal drug absorption.

### Strategy

Drug + Bile Acid Conjugate  
↓  
Recognized by ASBT  
↓  
Improved Uptake  
↓  
Enhanced Bioavailability

---

## 3. Hypercholesterolemia Treatment

Blocking ASBT reduces bile acid reabsorption.

### Result

- Increased bile acid excretion.
    
- Increased cholesterol utilization.
    
- Reduced plasma cholesterol.
    

---

# ASBT Inhibitors

Used in:

- Progressive Familial Intrahepatic Cholestasis (PFIC)
    

---

### Maralixibat

Used in:

- Alagille Syndrome
    

---

ASBT Inhibition  
↓  
Reduced Bile Acid Reabsorption  
↓  
Increased Fecal Excretion  
↓  
Reduced Liver Bile Acid Load

# Computational Modeling of ASBT

Transport kinetics generally follow **Michaelis-Menten kinetics**.

### Equation

J = (Jmax × C)/(Km + C)

Where:

- J = Transport rate
    
- Jmax = Maximum transport rate
    
- Km = Michaelis constant
    
- C = Substrate concentration

# Conclusion

ASBT (Apical Sodium-Dependent Bile Acid Transporter) is a sodium-dependent influx transporter encoded by the SLC10A2 gene and located mainly in the terminal ileum. It plays a vital role in bile acid reabsorption and enterohepatic circulation. ASBT is important for lipid digestion, vitamin absorption, drug delivery, and treatment of cholestatic liver diseases. It is therefore an important transporter in computational drug disposition and pharmaceutical development.
