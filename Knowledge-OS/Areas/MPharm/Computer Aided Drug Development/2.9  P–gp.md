# P-glycoprotein (P-gp / MDR1 / ABCB1) (10 Marks)

## Introduction

**P-glycoprotein (P-gp)** is an ATP-dependent efflux transporter that pumps drugs out of cells against the concentration gradient. It is one of the most important transport proteins involved in drug absorption, distribution, excretion, multidrug resistance (MDR), and drug-drug interactions. P-gp acts as a biological defense mechanism by preventing toxic substances from accumulating inside cells.

---

# Definition

**P-glycoprotein (P-gp)** is an ATP-Binding Cassette (ABC) transporter encoded by the **ABCB1 (MDR1)** gene that actively transports drugs from inside the cell to the extracellular space.

# Location of P-gp

P-gp is present on the apical membrane of:

1. Intestinal epithelium
    
2. Liver (hepatocytes)
    
3. Kidney tubules
    
4. Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB)
    
5. Placenta
    

---

# Mechanism of Action

### Step 1

Drug enters the cell.

### Step 2

P-gp recognizes the drug.

### Step 3

ATP is hydrolyzed.

### Step 4

Drug is pumped out of the cell.

### Step 5

Intracellular drug concentration decreases.

```text
Drug enters cell
        ↓
    P-gp binds
        ↓
 ATP Hydrolysis
        ↓
 Drug Efflux
        ↓
 Reduced Drug Level
```

# Functions of P-gp

### 1. Limits Intestinal Drug Absorption

P-gp pumps drugs back into the intestinal lumen, reducing absorption.

### 2. Protects Brain

At BBB, P-gp prevents drugs from entering the CNS.

### 3. Promotes Drug Excretion

Facilitates elimination through liver and kidneys.

### 4. Prevents Toxicity

Protects tissues from harmful substances.

---

# Clinical Importance

## 1. Digoxin Toxicity

P-gp inhibition by quinidine increases digoxin absorption and plasma concentration, leading to toxicity.

## 2. Loperamide Example

Loperamide is highly lipophilic but normally does not produce CNS effects because P-gp pumps it out of the brain.

## 3. Cancer Drug Resistance

Cancer cells overexpress P-gp and actively remove anticancer drugs.

This causes:

- Multidrug Resistance (MDR)
    
- Reduced chemotherapy effectiveness
    

---

# Role in Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB)

P-gp is highly expressed at BBB.

### Function

- Pumps drugs from brain back to blood.
    
- Limits CNS penetration.
    

### Example

Loperamide:

- High lipophilicity
    
- Should enter brain by passive diffusion
    
- P-gp prevents CNS entry
    

Therefore:

**Good Lipophilicity ≠ Good Brain Penetration**

# Computational Modeling of P-gp

P-gp transport follows **Michaelis-Menten kinetics**.

### Transport Equation

J = (Jmax × C) / (Km + C)

Where:

- J = Transport rate
    
- Jmax = Maximum transport rate
    
- Km = Michaelis constant
    
- C = Drug concentration

- GastroPlus
    
- Simcyp
    
- PBPK Models
    

Used for:

- BBB penetration prediction

# Conclusion

P-glycoprotein (P-gp) is an ATP-dependent efflux transporter encoded by the ABCB1 gene. It plays a critical role in drug absorption, distribution, BBB protection, drug excretion, and multidrug resistance. Because of its major influence on pharmacokinetics and drug-drug interactions, P-gp is routinely incorporated into modern PBPK and computational drug disposition models.
