# OCT (Organic Cation Transporters) (10 Marks)

## Introduction

**Organic Cation Transporters (OCTs)** are membrane transport proteins responsible for the uptake and transport of positively charged (cationic) drugs and endogenous compounds across biological membranes. They belong to the **Solute Carrier (SLC22) family** and play a major role in drug absorption, distribution, and excretion. OCTs are particularly important in the liver and kidneys, where they influence drug disposition and drug-drug interactions.

---

# Definition

**Organic Cation Transporters (OCTs)** are carrier-mediated influx transporters that facilitate the movement of positively charged organic compounds across cell membranes.

---

# Family and Types

OCTs belong to the **SLC22 transporter family**.

### Major Types

1. OCT1 (SLC22A1)
    
2. OCT2 (SLC22A2)
    
3. OCT3 (SLC22A3)
    

---

# Location of OCTs

|Transporter|Major Location|
|---|---|
|OCT1|Liver|
|OCT2|Kidney|
|OCT3|Heart, Brain, Liver, Skeletal Muscle|

---

# Mechanism of Transport

### Characteristics

- Carrier-mediated transport.
    
- Facilitated diffusion.
    
- Energy independent.
    
- Bidirectional transport.
    

### Process

```text
Blood
  ↓
Cationic Drug
  ↓
   OCT
  ↓
Inside Cell
```

OCTs transport positively charged molecules according to electrochemical gradients.

# OCT1 (SLC22A1)

## Location

Primarily in the liver.

## Function

- Hepatic uptake of cationic drugs.
    
- Facilitates drug metabolism.
    

## Important Drug

Metformin

### Clinical Importance

Reduced OCT1 activity may decrease metformin uptake into hepatocytes and reduce therapeutic effect.

---

# OCT2 (SLC22A2)

## Location

Basolateral membrane of kidney proximal tubules.

## Function

- Renal uptake of cationic drugs.
    
- Initiates renal secretion.
    

## Important Drugs

- Metformin
    
- Cisplatin
    
- Cimetidine
    

### Clinical Importance

Important in drug elimination and nephrotoxicity.

---

# OCT3 (SLC22A3)

## Location

- Heart
    
- Brain
    
- Liver
    
- Skeletal Muscle
    

## Function

- Wide tissue distribution.
    
- Transport of neurotransmitters and drugs.
    

---

# Clinical Importance

## 1. Metformin Disposition

Metformin is one of the most important OCT substrates.

### OCT1

Liver uptake

### OCT2

Renal uptake and elimination

Changes in OCT activity can significantly alter metformin pharmacokinetics.

---

## 2. Cisplatin Nephrotoxicity

OCT2 transports cisplatin into kidney cells.

Increased uptake may lead to:

- Renal toxicity
    
- Kidney damage
    

---

## 3. Drug-Drug Interactions

Example:

Metformin + Cimetidine

Cimetidine inhibits OCT-mediated transport resulting in increased metformin plasma levels.

---

# Computational Modeling of OCT

OCT transport follows **Michaelis-Menten kinetics**.

### Equation

J = (Jmax × C)/(Km + C)

Where:

- J = Transport rate
    
- Jmax = Maximum transport rate
    
- Km = Michaelis constant
    
- C = Drug concentration

# Conclusion

Organic Cation Transporters (OCTs) are important influx transporters belonging to the SLC22 family that mediate the transport of positively charged drugs and endogenous compounds. OCT1 is mainly involved in hepatic uptake, OCT2 in renal elimination, and OCT3 in tissue distribution. They play a critical role in drug disposition, drug-drug interactions, toxicity, and pharmacokinetic modeling.
