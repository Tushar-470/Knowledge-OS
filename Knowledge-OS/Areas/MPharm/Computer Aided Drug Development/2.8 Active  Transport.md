# Active Transport in Drug Disposition (10 Marks)

## Introduction

**Active transport** is the movement of drugs across biological membranes with the help of specific transporter proteins. Unlike passive diffusion, active transport is **carrier-mediated, selective, saturable, and energy-dependent (directly or indirectly)**. Active transport plays a major role in drug absorption, distribution, excretion, drug-drug interactions, and bioavailability.

---

# Types of Important Drug Transporters

## 1. P-glycoprotein (P-gp)

### Gene

ABCB1 (MDR1)

### Type

Efflux transporter

### Location

- Intestine
    
- Liver
    
- Kidney
    
- Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB)
    

### Function

Pumps drugs out of cells and reduces drug absorption.

### Important Substrates

- Digoxin
    
- Loperamide
    
- Doxorubicin
    
- Paclitaxel
    

### Importance

- Causes multidrug resistance (MDR)
    
- Limits CNS drug entry
    
- Responsible for many DDIs
    

---

## 2. BCRP (Breast Cancer Resistance Protein)

### Gene

ABCG2

### Type

Efflux transporter

### Location

- Intestine
    
- Liver
    
- Placenta
    
- BBB
    

### Function

Pumps drugs and metabolites out of cells.

### Important Substrates

- Rosuvastatin
    
- Methotrexate
    
- Gefitinib
    
- Imatinib
    

### Importance

- Drug resistance in cancer
    
- Affects statin disposition
    

---

## 3. Nucleoside Transporters (ENT/CNT)

### Types

#### ENT (Equilibrative Nucleoside Transporters)

- ENT1
    
- ENT2
    

Facilitated diffusion transporters.

#### CNT (Concentrative Nucleoside Transporters)

- CNT1
    
- CNT2
    
- CNT3
    

Active influx transporters.

### Function

Transport nucleosides and nucleoside analog drugs into cells.

### Important Drugs

- Gemcitabine
    
- Cytarabine
    
- Ribavirin
    
- Zidovudine
    

### Importance

Essential for uptake of antiviral and anticancer drugs.

---

## 4. hPEPT1 (Human Peptide Transporter-1)

### Gene

SLC15A1

### Type

Influx transporter

### Location

Apical membrane of small intestine

### Function

Absorbs dipeptides, tripeptides, and peptide-like drugs.

### Important Drugs

- Valacyclovir
    
- Amoxicillin
    
- Cephalexin
    
- Enalapril
    

### Importance

Improves oral bioavailability of peptide-like drugs.

Example:

Valacyclovir bioavailability ≈ 54%

Acyclovir bioavailability ≈ 10–20%

---

## 5. ASBT (Apical Sodium-Dependent Bile Acid Transporter)

### Gene

SLC10A2

### Type

Influx transporter

### Location

Terminal ileum

### Function

Reabsorbs bile acids from intestine.

### Importance

- Enterohepatic circulation
    
- Drug recycling
    
- Target for liver disease treatment
    

### Example Drugs

- Odevixibat
    
- Maralixibat
    

---

## 6. OCT (Organic Cation Transporters)

### Types

- OCT1
    
- OCT2
    
- OCT3
    

### Family

SLC22

### Function

Transport positively charged drugs.

### OCT1

Location:

- Liver
    

Drug:

- Metformin
    

### OCT2

Location:

- Kidney
    

Drug:

- Metformin
    
- Cisplatin
    

### Importance

Controls hepatic uptake and renal secretion of cationic drugs.

---

## 7. OATP (Organic Anion Transporting Polypeptides)

### Types

- OATP1B1
    
- OATP1B3
    
- OATP2B1
    

### Function

Transport large organic anions into cells.

### Location

Mainly liver

### Important Drugs

- Pravastatin
    
- Rosuvastatin
    
- Atorvastatin
    

### Importance

- Hepatic drug uptake
    
- Statin disposition
    
- Drug-drug interactions
    

---

## 8. BBB Choline Transporter

### Location

Blood-Brain Barrier

### Function

Transports choline from blood to brain.

### Importance

- Acetylcholine synthesis
    
- CNS drug delivery
    
- Brain function
    

### Example

Choline is transported into the brain through specific transporter systems present at BBB.
