# Drug Interactions (10 Marks)

## Definition

A **drug interaction** occurs when the pharmacological or pharmacokinetic effect of one drug is altered by the presence of another drug, food, or chemical substance. Drug interactions may increase or decrease the therapeutic effect or toxicity of a drug.

---

# Classification of Drug Interactions

## 1. Pharmacokinetic (PK) Interactions

One drug alters the **ADME** of another drug.

### Affected Processes

- Absorption
    
- Distribution
    
- Metabolism
    
- Excretion
    

### Result

Changes plasma drug concentration.

---

## 2. Pharmacodynamic (PD) Interactions

One drug alters the effect of another at the receptor or organ level without changing plasma concentration.

### Result

- Synergism
    
- Antagonism
    

### Example

- Aspirin + Warfarin → Increased bleeding
    

---

## 3. Pharmaceutical Interactions

Occurs before administration due to physical or chemical incompatibility.

### Example

- Drug precipitation in IV solutions
    

---

# Pharmacokinetic Drug Interactions

## A. Absorption Interactions

### Mechanism

One drug affects absorption of another.

### Examples

- Antacids + Tetracycline
    
- Iron + Ciprofloxacin
    

### Result

↓ Drug absorption and ↓ bioavailability

---

## B. Distribution Interactions

### Protein Binding Displacement

One drug displaces another from plasma protein binding sites.

### Examples

#### Warfarin + Aspirin

- Aspirin displaces warfarin from albumin.
    
- Increased free warfarin.
    
- Increased bleeding risk.
    

#### Valproic Acid + Phenytoin

- Increased free phenytoin.
    
- Neurotoxicity may occur.
    

---

## C. Metabolism Interactions (Most Important)

### Enzyme Inhibition

One drug inhibits CYP450 enzymes.

### Examples

- Erythromycin + Theophylline
    
- Ketoconazole + Cyclosporine
    

### Result

- Metabolism decreases
    
- Plasma concentration increases
    
- Toxicity increases
    

---

### Enzyme Induction

One drug increases CYP450 enzyme activity.

### Examples

- Rifampicin
    
- Carbamazepine
    
- Phenytoin
    

### Result

- Metabolism increases
    
- Plasma concentration decreases
    
- Therapeutic effect decreases
    

---

## D. Excretion Interactions

### Mechanism

One drug alters renal excretion of another.

### Examples

- Probenecid + Penicillin
    
- Probenecid + Methotrexate
    

### Result

- Reduced renal clearance
    
- Increased plasma concentration

# Clinical Significance

1. May cause toxicity.
    
2. May reduce therapeutic effect.
    
3. Important in elderly patients.
    
4. Important in patients receiving multiple drugs.
    
5. Requires dose adjustment and monitoring.
    

---

# Conclusion

Drug interactions occur when one drug alters the effect of another. They are classified into **pharmacokinetic, pharmacodynamic, and pharmaceutical interactions**. Among them, **metabolic interactions involving CYP450 enzymes are the most clinically significant** and require careful monitoring.
