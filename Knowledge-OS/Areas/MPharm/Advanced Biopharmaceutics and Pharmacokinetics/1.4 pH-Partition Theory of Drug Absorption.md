# pH-Partition Theory of Drug Absorption (10 Marks)

## Definition

The **pH-partition theory** states that **drugs are absorbed through biological membranes mainly in their unionized (lipid-soluble) form**, whereas ionized drugs are absorbed poorly. Drug absorption depends on the **pH of the medium and the pKa of the drug**.

---

# Principle

Biological membranes are lipid in nature.

### Therefore:

✅ Unionized drug → Lipid soluble → Easily absorbed

❌ Ionized drug → Water soluble → Poorly absorbed

---

# Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation

## For Weak Acids

[  
pH = pKa + \log \frac{A^-}{HA}  
]

Where:

- HA = Unionized acid
    
- A⁻ = Ionized acid
    

### Weak Acid

- Low pH → More unionized
    
- Better absorption
    

### Example

- Aspirin
    
- Phenobarbital
    

---

## For Weak Bases

[  
pH = pKa + \log \frac{B}{BH^+}  
]

Where:

- B = Unionized base
    
- BH⁺ = Ionized base
    

### Weak Base

- High pH → More unionized
    
- Better absorption
    

### Example

- Morphine
    
- Quinidine
    

---

# Drug Absorption According to pH-Partition Theory

## Weak Acid

### In Stomach (pH 1–3)

```text
HA  ⇌  H+ + A-
(Unionized)
```

- Mostly unionized
    
- Better absorption
    

### Example

- Aspirin
    

---

## Weak Base

### In Intestine (pH 6–8)

```text
BH+  ⇌  H+ + B
         (Unionized)
```

- More unionized
    
- Better absorption
    

### Example

- Quinidine

# Limitations of pH-Partition Theory

The theory cannot explain:

### 1. Absorption of Highly Ionized Drugs

Example:

- Sulfathiazole
    

### 2. Absorption from Small Intestine

According to theory:

- Weak acids should be absorbed mainly from stomach.
    

In reality:

- Most drugs are absorbed from small intestine due to enormous surface area.
    

### 3. Carrier-Mediated Transport

Cannot explain:

- Levodopa
    
- Glucose
    
- Amino acids
    

### 4. Active Transport and Efflux Systems

Cannot explain:

- P-glycoprotein (P-gp)
    
- PEPT1 transporters
    

### 5. Influence of Blood Flow and Surface Area

Theory considers only pH and pKa.

---

# Clinical Significance

- Helps predict site of absorption.
    
- Helps design oral dosage forms.
    
- Useful in understanding bioavailability.
    
- Basis for enteric-coated formulations.
    

---

# Conclusion

The pH-partition theory states that only the **unionized form of a drug can readily cross biological membranes**. Weak acids are absorbed better in acidic media, whereas weak bases are absorbed better in alkaline media. Although useful, the theory has limitations because drug absorption is also influenced by surface area, blood flow, and transporters.

### Viva Question

**Why are most drugs absorbed in the small intestine despite the pH-partition theory?**

**Answer:** Because the small intestine has a very large surface area, villi, microvilli, and rich blood supply. ✅
