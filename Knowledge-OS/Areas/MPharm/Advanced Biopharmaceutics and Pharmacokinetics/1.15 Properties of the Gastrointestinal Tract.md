# Properties of the Gastrointestinal Tract (GIT) (10 Marks)

## Introduction

The **Gastrointestinal Tract (GIT)** is the major site for absorption of orally administered drugs. Its anatomical and physiological properties greatly influence the rate and extent of drug absorption.

---

# 1. Large Surface Area

The small intestine contains:

- Circular folds
    
- Villi
    
- Microvilli
    

These structures greatly increase the absorptive surface area.

### Importance

- Provides large area for drug absorption.
    
- Enhances bioavailability.
    

### Key Point

✅ Small intestine surface area ≈ **200 m²**

```text
Folds
  ↓

```

---

# 2. pH Variation Along the GIT

Different parts of GIT have different pH values.

|Region|pH|
|---|---|
|Stomach|1–3|
|Duodenum|5–6|
|Jejunum|6–7|
|Ileum|7–8|
|Colon|6–7|

### Importance

- Affects drug ionization.
    
- Influences pH-partition hypothesis.
    
- Determines site of absorption.
    

---

# 3. Rich Blood Supply

The intestine has extensive blood circulation.

### Importance

- Maintains concentration gradient.
    
- Rapidly removes absorbed drug.
    
- Increases absorption rate.
    

✅ More blood flow = Better absorption

---

# 4. Membrane Permeability

The intestinal membrane acts as a selective barrier.

### Importance

- Lipid-soluble drugs cross easily.
    
- Hydrophilic drugs cross with difficulty.
    

### Examples

- Diazepam → High permeability
    
- Atenolol → Low permeability
    

---

# 5. Gastrointestinal Motility

Movement of food and drug through GIT by peristalsis.

### Importance

- Controls contact time with absorptive surface.
    
- Excessive motility decreases absorption.
    

### Example

- Diarrhea → Reduced absorption
    

---

# 6. Gastric Emptying Time

Time required for stomach contents to enter the small intestine.

### Importance

- Faster emptying → Faster absorption.
    
- Delayed emptying → Slower absorption.
    

### Example

- Fatty meals delay gastric emptying.
    

---

# 7. Presence of Transporters and Enzymes

Important transporters:

- PEPT1
    
- P-glycoprotein (P-gp)
    

Important enzymes:

- CYP3A4
    

### Importance

- Influence drug uptake.
    
- Cause drug-drug interactions.
    
- Affect bioavailability.
    

---

# 8. First-Pass Metabolism

After absorption, drugs enter portal circulation and pass through the liver.

### Importance

- Some drugs are metabolized before reaching systemic circulation.
    
- Reduces bioavailability.
    

### Examples

- Propranolol
    
- Morphine
    
- Nitroglycerin

# Conclusion

The major properties of the GIT affecting drug absorption are **large surface area, pH variation, rich blood supply, membrane permeability, motility, gastric emptying, transporters, and first-pass metabolism**. Among these, the **large surface area of the small intestine makes it the principal site of drug absorption.**
