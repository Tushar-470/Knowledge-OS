# Tight Junction Complex (10 Marks)

## Definition

The **Tight Junction Complex (TJ)** is a specialized protein structure present between adjacent intestinal epithelial cells. It forms a selective barrier that regulates the movement of substances through the **paracellular pathway** (between cells).

---

# Location

- Present at the apical region of epithelial cells.
    
- Found throughout the gastrointestinal tract.
    
- Connects neighboring intestinal cells.
    

```text
Cell A  │TJ│  Cell B
         ↑
 Tight Junction
```

---

# Structure of Tight Junction Complex

The tight junction is composed of several proteins:

### 1. Claudins

- Major structural proteins.
    
- Control pore size and permeability.
    

### 2. Occludin

- Maintains junction integrity.
    
- Regulates barrier function.
    

### 3. ZO Proteins (ZO-1, ZO-2, ZO-3)

- Link tight junction proteins to the cytoskeleton.
    

### 4. JAM (Junctional Adhesion Molecules)

- Help in cell-cell adhesion.

# Functions of Tight Junctions

## 1. Barrier Function

- Prevents entry of harmful substances.
    
- Maintains intestinal integrity.
    

## 2. Regulates Paracellular Transport

- Controls movement of water and small molecules between cells.
    

## 3. Maintains Cell Polarity

- Separates apical and basolateral membranes.
    

## 4. Controls Drug Absorption

- Influences absorption of hydrophilic drugs.
    

---

# Role in Drug Absorption

### Paracellular Transport

Small water-soluble drugs may pass through tight junction pores.

### Examples

- Mannitol
    
- Atenolol
    
- Certain peptides
    

```text
Intestinal Lumen
        │
        ▼
   Tight Junction
        │
        ▼
 Blood Circulation
```

---

# Factors Affecting Tight Junction Permeability

### Increase Permeability

- Absorption enhancers
    
- EDTA
    
- Chitosan
    
- Some surfactants
    

### Decrease Permeability

- Healthy epithelial barrier
    
- Tight claudin organization
    

---

# Importance in Drug Delivery

1. Important for oral absorption of hydrophilic drugs.
    
2. Target for peptide and protein delivery.
    
3. Used in formulation design to improve absorption.
    
4. Influences bioavailability.
    

---

# Conclusion

The **Tight Junction Complex** is a protein network consisting mainly of **claudins, occludin, ZO proteins, and JAM proteins**. It regulates paracellular transport, maintains epithelial barrier integrity, and plays an important role in gastrointestinal drug absorption, especially for hydrophilic drugs.
