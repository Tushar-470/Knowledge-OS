# 1. Dissolution Rate (10 Marks)

## Definition

**Dissolution rate** is the amount of drug dissolved per unit time under specified conditions of temperature, pH, and solvent composition. It determines how fast a drug goes into solution before absorption.

---

## Noyes-Whitney Equation

 
\frac{dC}{dt}

\frac{D \times A \times (C_s-C)}  
{h \times V}  
]

Where:

- dC/dt = Dissolution rate
    
- D = Diffusion coefficient
    
- A = Surface area
    
- Cs = Saturation solubility
    
- C = Drug concentration in bulk solution
    
- h = Diffusion layer thickness
    
- V = Volume of dissolution medium
    

---

## Factors Affecting Dissolution Rate

### Increase Dissolution Rate

✅ Smaller particle size

✅ Larger surface area

✅ Higher solubility

✅ Better stirring/agitation

✅ Higher temperature

### Decrease Dissolution Rate

❌ Large particles

❌ Poor wettability

❌ Hydrophobic excipients

---

## Importance

- Faster dissolution → Faster absorption
    
- Important for BCS Class II drugs
    
- Improves bioavailability
    

---

## Conclusion

Dissolution rate determines how quickly a drug enters solution and becomes available for absorption. It is explained by the Noyes-Whitney equation.

# 2. Dissolution Process (10 Marks)

## Definition

**Dissolution process** is the mechanism by which a solid drug dissolves in a solvent to form a solution.

It occurs in **two steps**.

---

## Steps in Dissolution Process

### Step 1: Interfacial Reaction

```text
Solid Drug
     ↓
Drug molecules separate
from crystal surface
```

- Drug molecules break away from crystal lattice.
    
- Solvent wets the drug surface.
    

---

### Step 2: Diffusion Step

```text
Drug Surface
      ↓
Diffusion Layer
      ↓
Bulk Solution
```

- Drug molecules diffuse through the stagnant diffusion layer into bulk solution.
    
- Usually the rate-limiting step.
    

---

## Diffusion Layer Model

```text
Solid Surface
     │ Cs
     │
     │ Diffusion Layer (h)
     │
Bulk Solution (C)
```

Where:

- Cs = Saturation concentration
    
- C = Bulk concentration
    
- h = Diffusion layer thickness
    

---

## Mechanism Summary

```text
Solid Drug
     ↓
Wetting
     ↓
Solvation
     ↓
Diffusion Layer
     ↓
Bulk Solution
     ↓
Absorption
```

---

## Importance

1. Necessary before absorption.
    
2. Controls bioavailability.
    
3. Critical for poorly soluble drugs.
    
4. Basis of dissolution testing.
    

---

## Conclusion

The dissolution process involves **interfacial solvation of drug particles followed by diffusion through the diffusion layer into the bulk solution**. The diffusion step is usually the rate-controlling step.
