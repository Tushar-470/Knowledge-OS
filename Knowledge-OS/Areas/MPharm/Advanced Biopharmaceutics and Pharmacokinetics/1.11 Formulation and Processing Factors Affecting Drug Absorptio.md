# Formulation and Processing Factors Affecting Drug Absorption (10 Marks)

## Introduction

**Formulation factors** and **processing factors** significantly affect the dissolution rate, drug release, absorption, and bioavailability of oral dosage forms. Even if two products contain the same drug, differences in formulation and manufacturing can produce different bioavailability.

---

# I. Formulation Factors

## 1. Diluents (Fillers)

### Examples

- Lactose
    
- Mannitol
    
- Microcrystalline Cellulose (MCC)
    

### Effect

- Improve tablet wetting.
    
- Enhance disintegration and dissolution.
    

Hydrophilic diluents generally increase dissolution.

---

## 2. Disintegrants

### Examples

- Crospovidone
    
- Croscarmellose Sodium
    
- Sodium Starch Glycolate
    

### Effect

- Help tablet break into smaller particles.
    
- Increase surface area.
    
- Increase dissolution and absorption.
    

---

## 3. Lubricants

### Example

- Magnesium Stearate
    

### Effect

Excess lubricant causes:

- Hydrophobic coating around particles
    
- Poor wetting
    
- Slower dissolution
    

Result:

❌ Reduced absorption

---

## 4. Surfactants

### Example

- Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS)
    

### Effect

- Improve wettability.
    
- Increase solubility.
    
- Increase dissolution.
    

---

## 5. Binders

### Examples

- PVP
    
- Starch paste
    

### Effect

- Increase tablet strength.
    
- Excess binder may retard dissolution.
    

---

## 6. Coating

### Types

- Film coating
    
- Enteric coating
    

### Effect

- Protects drug.
    
- May delay drug release and absorption.
    

---

# II. Processing Factors

## 1. Compression Force

### Effect

High compression:

- Hard tablet
    
- Reduced porosity
    
- Slower disintegration
    
- Slower dissolution
    

Low compression:

- Faster disintegration
    

---

## 2. Particle Size of Drug

### Effect

Smaller particles:

- Larger surface area
    
- Faster dissolution
    
- Better absorption
    

### Example

- Micronized drugs
    

---

## 3. Granulation Method

### Types

- Wet granulation
    
- Dry granulation
    
- Direct compression
    

### Effect

Granule properties affect:

- Disintegration
    
- Dissolution
    
- Bioavailability
    

---

## 4. Mixing and Blending

### Effect

Proper mixing:

- Uniform drug distribution
    

Overmixing:

- Excess lubricant coating
    
- Reduced dissolution
    

---

## 5. Drying Conditions

### Effect

Excess drying:

- Hard granules
    
- Poor disintegration
    

Result:

- Reduced dissolution rate
    

---

1. Affect dissolution rate.
    
2. Affect bioavailability.
    
3. Ensure batch-to-batch consistency.
    
4. Improve therapeutic effectiveness.
    
5. Important in product development.

# Conclusion

Formulation factors such as **diluents, disintegrants, lubricants, surfactants, binders, and coatings**, along with processing factors such as **compression force, particle size, granulation, mixing, and drying**, significantly influence drug dissolution and absorption. Proper optimization ensures maximum bioavailability.
