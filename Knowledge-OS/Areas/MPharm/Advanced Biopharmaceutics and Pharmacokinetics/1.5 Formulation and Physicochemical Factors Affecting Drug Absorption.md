# Formulation and Physicochemical Factors Affecting Drug Absorption (10 Marks)

## Introduction

Drug absorption and bioavailability are influenced by two major groups of factors:

1. **Physicochemical Factors** (related to drug properties)
    
2. **Formulation Factors** (related to dosage form and excipients)
    

These factors affect dissolution, permeability, and absorption of drugs.

---

# I. Physicochemical Factors

## 1. Lipid Solubility

- Lipid-soluble drugs cross biological membranes easily.
    
- Higher lipid solubility → Higher absorption.
    

### Example

- Diazepam
    

---

## 2. Degree of Ionization (pKa)

- Unionized drugs are absorbed better.
    
- Depends on pH-partition theory.
    

### Example

- Aspirin absorbed in stomach.
    
- Weak bases absorbed in intestine.
    

---

## 3. Molecular Size

- Small molecules absorb easily.
    
- Large molecules show poor absorption.
    

### Example

- Insulin is poorly absorbed orally.
    

---

## 4. Solubility

- Drug must dissolve before absorption.
    
- Higher solubility → Better absorption.
    

---

## 5. Dissolution Rate

According to Noyes-Whitney equation:

[  
\frac{dC}{dt}=\frac{DA(Cs-C)}{hV}  
]

Faster dissolution leads to faster absorption.

---

## 6. Particle Size

- Smaller particle size increases surface area.
    
- Faster dissolution and absorption.
    

### Example

- Micronized drugs
    

---

# II. Formulation Factors

## 1. Diluents (Fillers)

### Hydrophilic Diluents

- Lactose
    
- Mannitol
    
- MCC
    

Increase wetting and dissolution.

### Hydrophobic Diluents

- Stearic acid
    
- Waxes
    

Decrease dissolution and absorption.

---

## 2. Disintegrants

Help tablet break into smaller particles.

### Examples

- Crospovidone
    
- Croscarmellose sodium
    
- Sodium starch glycolate
    

Result:

- Faster disintegration
    
- Increased surface area
    
- Faster absorption
    

---

## 3. Lubricants

### Example

- Magnesium stearate
    

Excess lubricant forms hydrophobic coating around particles.

### Result

- Reduced wetting
    
- Reduced dissolution
    
- Reduced absorption
    

---

## 4. Surfactants

### Example

- Sodium lauryl sulfate (SLS)
    

Increase wetting and solubility.

### Result

- Improved dissolution
    
- Improved absorption
    

---

## 5. Binders

### Examples

- PVP
    
- Starch paste
    

Excess binder may retard drug release.

---

## 6. Dosage Form

Rate of absorption:

```text
Solution > Suspension > Capsule > Tablet
```

Solutions are absorbed fastest.

# Conclusion

Drug absorption is affected by both **physicochemical properties of the drug** and **formulation factors of the dosage form**. Important physicochemical factors include lipid solubility, pKa, solubility, and particle size, while major formulation factors include diluents, disintegrants, lubricants, surfactants, and dosage form. Proper optimization of these factors improves bioavailability.
