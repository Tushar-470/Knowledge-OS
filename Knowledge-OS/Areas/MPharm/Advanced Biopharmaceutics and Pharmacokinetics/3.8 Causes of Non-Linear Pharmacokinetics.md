# Causes of Non-Linear Pharmacokinetics (10 Marks)

## Definition

Non-linear pharmacokinetics occurs when one or more pharmacokinetic parameters (**CL, Vd, F, t½**) change with dose or concentration. It results from saturation of physiological processes involved in ADME.

---

# Causes of Non-Linearity

## 1. Saturable Metabolism (Most Common Cause)

When drug-metabolizing enzymes become saturated, the rate of metabolism no longer increases proportionally with concentration.

### Michaelis-Menten Equation

[  
v=\frac{V_{max}\times C}{K_m+C}  
]

### Examples

- Phenytoin
    
- Salicylates
    
- Ethanol
    

### Result

- Clearance decreases
    
- Half-life increases
    
- Small dose increase → large increase in plasma concentration
    

---

## 2. Saturable Plasma Protein Binding

At higher concentrations, plasma protein binding sites become saturated.

### Result

- Free drug fraction increases
    
- Volume of distribution changes
    
- Clearance changes non-linearly
    

### Examples

- Warfarin
    
- Valproic acid
    
- Phenylbutazone
    

---

## 3. Saturable Absorption

Carrier-mediated absorption processes become saturated at high doses.

### Result

- Bioavailability decreases
    
- AUC increases less than proportionally
    

### Examples

- Gabapentin
    
- Levodopa
    
- Riboflavin
    

---

## 4. Saturable First-Pass Metabolism

Metabolic enzymes in the liver or intestine become saturated.

### Result

- Bioavailability increases
    
- AUC increases more than proportionally
    

### Examples

- Propranolol
    
- Verapamil
    

---

## 5. Saturable Renal Tubular Secretion

Renal transporters become saturated at high drug concentrations.

### Result

- Renal clearance decreases
    
- Drug accumulates in plasma
    

### Examples

- Para-aminohippuric acid (PAH)
    
- Methotrexate
    
- Probenecid interactions
    

---

## 6. Enzyme Autoinduction

Drug induces its own metabolism with continued administration.

### Result

- Clearance increases with time
    
- Plasma concentration decreases
    

### Examples

- Carbamazepine
    
- Rifampicin

# 30-Second Revision

### 6 Causes

1. **Saturable Metabolism** → Phenytoin
    
2. **Saturable Protein Binding** → Warfarin
    
3. **Saturable Absorption** → Gabapentin
    
4. **Saturable First-Pass Metabolism** → Propranolol
    
5. **Saturable Renal Secretion** → PAH
    
6. **Autoinduction** → Carbamazepine
    

### Mnemonic

**"MAPS RA"**

- **M** = Metabolism
    
- **A** = Absorption
    
- **P** = Protein binding
    
- **S** = Saturable first-pass metabolism
    
- **R** = Renal secretion
    
- **A** = Autoinduction
