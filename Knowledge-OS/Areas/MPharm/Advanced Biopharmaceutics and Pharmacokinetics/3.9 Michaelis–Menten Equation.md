# Michaelis–Menten Equation (10 Marks)

## Definition

The **Michaelis–Menten equation** describes the rate of metabolism of drugs when the metabolizing enzyme becomes saturated. It is used to explain **non-linear (capacity-limited) pharmacokinetics**.

---

# Enzyme Reaction

```text
E + S  ⇌  ES  →  E + P
```

Where:

- E = Enzyme
    
- S = Substrate (Drug)
    
- ES = Enzyme-Substrate Complex
    
- P = Product (Metabolite)
    

---

# Michaelis–Menten Equation

$$
v = \frac{V_{\max} \times C}{K_m + C}
$$


Where:

- **v** = Rate of drug metabolism
    
- **Vmax** = Maximum metabolic rate
    
- **Km** = Michaelis constant
    
- **C** = Drug concentration
    

### Important Point

[  
C = K_m  
]

then

$$
v = \frac{V_{\max}}{2}
$$

Thus, **Km is the concentration at which metabolism rate becomes half of Vmax.**

---

# Pharmacokinetic Form

For drug elimination:

$$
\frac{dC}{dt}
=
-\frac{V_{\max}\,C}{K_m + C}
$$


This equation explains saturable drug elimination.

---

# Behavior at Different Drug Concentrations

## 1. When C << Km

$$
v \approx \frac{V_{\max}}{K_m} \times C
$$


### Characteristics

- First-order kinetics
    
- Clearance constant
    
- Half-life constant
    
- AUC proportional to dose
    

### Example

- Most drugs at low concentration
    

---

## 2. When C ≈ Km

### Characteristics

- Mixed-order kinetics
    
- Non-linear region
    
- Small dose increase causes large concentration increase
    

### Example

- Therapeutic doses of Phenytoin
    

**This is the danger zone.**

---

## 3. When C >> Km

$$
v \approx V_{\max}
$$


### Characteristics

- Zero-order kinetics
    
- Elimination rate constant
    
- Clearance decreases
    
- Drug accumulation occurs
    

### Examples

- Ethanol
    
- Phenytoin toxicity
    
- High-dose salicylates
    

---

# Clinical Significance

### Phenytoin

- Follows Michaelis–Menten kinetics.
    
- Small dose increase may produce a very large rise in plasma concentration.
    
- Therapeutic drug monitoring is essential.
    

### Other Drugs

- Ethanol
    
- Salicylates
    

---

# Advantages

1. Explains saturable metabolism.
    
2. Predicts non-linear pharmacokinetics.
    
3. Helps calculate dosage adjustments.
    
4. Useful for therapeutic drug monitoring.
    

---

# Conclusion

The Michaelis–Menten equation,

$$
v=\frac{V_{\max}C}{K_m+C}
$$


describes enzyme-mediated drug metabolism. It explains the transition from **first-order kinetics (C << Km)** to **zero-order kinetics (C >> Km)** and forms the basis of non-linear pharmacokinetics. Phenytoin is the classic example.

### Formula

$$
v=\frac{V_{\max}C}{K_m+C}
$$


### Three Conditions

|Condition|Kinetics|
|---|---|
|C << Km|First-order|
|C ≈ Km|Mixed-order|
|C >> Km|Zero-order|

### Examples

- Phenytoin
    
- Ethanol
    
- Salicylates
