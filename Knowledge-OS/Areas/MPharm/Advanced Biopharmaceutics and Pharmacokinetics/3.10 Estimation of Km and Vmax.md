# Estimation of Km and Vmax (10 Marks)

## Definition

**Km (Michaelis constant)** and **Vmax (maximum metabolic rate)** are important parameters of the Michaelis-Menten equation used to characterize non-linear pharmacokinetics and enzyme kinetics.

### Michaelis-Menten Equation

$$
v=\frac{V_{\max}\times C}{K_m+C}
$$


Where:

- **v** = Rate of metabolism
    
- **Vmax** = Maximum metabolic rate
    
- **Km** = Michaelis constant
    
- **C** = Drug concentration
    

---

# Methods for Estimation of Km and Vmax

## 1. Lineweaver-Burk Plot (Double Reciprocal Plot)

Taking reciprocal of Michaelis-Menten equation:

$$
\frac{1}{v}
=
\frac{K_m}{V_{\max}}
\cdot
\frac{1}{C}
+
\frac{1}{V_{\max}}
$$


### Plot

```text
1/v
│
│      /
│    /
│  /
│/
└────────── 1/C
```

### Parameters

- Slope = Km/Vmax
    
- Y-intercept = 1/Vmax
    
- X-intercept = -1/Km
    

### Advantages

- Simple graphical method
    

### Limitation

- Amplifies experimental errors
    

---

## 2. Hanes-Woolf Plot

Equation:

$$
\frac{C}{v}
=
\frac{1}{V_{\max}}\,C
+
\frac{K_m}{V_{\max}}
$$


### Plot

```text
C/v
│
│    /
│   /
│  /
│ /
└───────── C
```

### Parameters

- Slope = 1/Vmax
    
- Y-intercept = Km/Vmax
    

### Advantage

- More accurate than Lineweaver-Burk.
    

---

## 3. Eadie-Hofstee Plot

Equation:

$$
v = -K_m\left(\frac{v}{C}\right) + V_{\max}
$$


### Plot

```text
v
│\
│ \
│  \
│   \
└──────── v/C
```

### Parameters

- Slope = −Km
    
- Y-intercept = Vmax
    

### Advantage

- Useful for detecting non-Michaelis-Menten behavior.
    

---

## 4. Direct Linear Plot (Eisenthal–Cornish-Bowden)

- Non-parametric graphical method.
    
- Lines are drawn from experimental points.
    
- Intersection point gives Km and Vmax.
    

### Advantage

- More robust and accurate.
    
- Recommended for clinical estimation.
    

---

## 5. Steady-State Method (Ritschel Method)

Most practical method for drugs like **Phenytoin**.

At steady state:

$$
R_0=
\frac{V_{\max}\times C_{ss}}
{K_m+C_{ss}}
$$


Where:

- R₀ = Dose rate
    
- Css = Steady-state concentration
    

Using two dose-Css pairs:

### Km

$$
K_m
=
\frac{
R_{02}C_{ss1}
-
R_{01}C_{ss2}
}{
R_{01}
-
R_{02}
}
$$


### Vmax

$$
V_{\max}
=
\frac{R_{01}\left(K_m + C_{ss1}\right)}
{C_{ss1}}
$$

# Conclusion

Km and Vmax are estimated using **Lineweaver-Burk, Hanes-Woolf, Eadie-Hofstee, Direct Linear Plot, and Steady-State methods**. Among these, the **Steady-State Method** is commonly used clinically for drugs such as **Phenytoin**, while graphical methods are useful for enzyme kinetic analysis.
