# Transport Model of Drug Absorption

## Introduction

Drug absorption through the gastrointestinal tract depends on:

1. Permeability
    
2. Solubility
    
3. Charge state (ionization)
    
4. GIT properties
    
5. Tight junctions and cellular environment
    

These factors determine the rate and extent of drug absorption.

---

# 1. Permeability–Solubility–Charge State and pH Partition Hypothesis

## pH Partition Hypothesis

**Only unionized (uncharged) drug molecules can easily cross lipid membranes.**

### Weak Acids

- Low pH → Unionized
    
- Better absorption
    

Example:

- Aspirin
    

### Weak Bases

- High pH → Unionized
    
- Better absorption
    

Example:

- Quinidine
    

```text
Unionized Drug
      ↓
Lipid Membrane
      ↓
Absorption
```

### Key Point

✅ Unionized = More Permeable

✅ Ionized = Less Permeable

---

# 2. Properties of Gastrointestinal Tract (GIT)

The small intestine is the major site of absorption because of:

### Large Surface Area

- Folds
    
- Villi
    
- Microvilli
    

### Rich Blood Supply

Maintains concentration gradient.

### Long Residence Time

Allows more absorption.

### Variable pH

|Region|pH|
|---|---|
|Stomach|1–3|
|Duodenum|5–6|
|Jejunum|6–7|
|Ileum|7–8|

### Important Point

✅ Jejunum = Main absorption site

---

# 3. pH Microclimate

## Definition

A thin layer of fluid exists near the intestinal surface having a pH different from the bulk intestinal fluid.

### Features

- Surface pH ≈ 5.5–6
    
- Bulk intestinal pH ≈ 6.5–7.5
    

### Importance

- Influences ionization of drugs.
    
- Affects drug absorption.
    

```text
Intestinal Lumen
      pH 7
        ↓
Microclimate
    pH 5.5-6
        ↓
Cell Membrane
```

---

# 4. Intracellular pH Environment

## Definition

The pH inside intestinal epithelial cells differs slightly from the lumen.

### Importance

- Influences drug ionization.
    
- Causes ion trapping.
    
- Affects weak acids and weak bases differently.
    

### Example

Weak bases may become ionized inside cells and become trapped.

```text
Drug enters Cell
        ↓
Ionization
        ↓
Drug Trapping
```

---

# 5. Tight Junction Complex

## Definition

Tight junctions are protein structures joining adjacent epithelial cells.

### Main Proteins

- Claudins
    
- Occludin
    
- ZO proteins
    

### Function

- Control paracellular transport.
    
- Prevent unwanted molecule entry.
    

### Importance

Small hydrophilic drugs may pass through tight junctions.

```text
Cell │TJ│ Cell
     ↑
 Tight Junction
```

### Examples

- Mannitol
    
- Atenolol

# Conclusion

Drug absorption depends on **permeability, solubility, charge state, GIT physiology, pH microclimate, intracellular pH, and tight junctions**. Among these, the **unionized form of a drug and the large surface area of the small intestine** are the most important determinants of gastrointestinal absorption.
