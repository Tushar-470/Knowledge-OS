# Drug Interactions Linked to Transporters (10 Marks)

## Introduction

Drug transporters are membrane proteins that control the movement of drugs across biological membranes. Transporter-mediated drug interactions occur when one drug inhibits or competes with the transporter responsible for the absorption, distribution, or excretion of another drug, resulting in altered drug concentrations and therapeutic effects.

---

# Major Drug Transporters

## 1. P-Glycoprotein (P-gp / MDR1)

### Function

- Efflux transporter
    
- Pumps drugs out of cells
    
- Present in intestine, liver, kidney, and blood-brain barrier
    

### Interaction

When P-gp is inhibited:

- Drug efflux decreases
    
- Drug absorption increases
    
- Plasma concentration increases
    

### Example

**Digoxin + Quinidine**

- Quinidine inhibits P-gp.
    
- Digoxin elimination decreases.
    
- Digoxin concentration increases.
    
- Digoxin toxicity occurs.
    

---

## 2. Organic Anion Transporting Polypeptides (OATPs)

### Function

- Uptake transporters
    
- Facilitate drug entry into hepatocytes and enterocytes
    

### Interaction

Inhibition of OATP:

- Drug uptake decreases
    
- Plasma drug concentration increases
    

### Example

**Statins + Cyclosporine**

- Cyclosporine inhibits OATP.
    
- Statin uptake into liver decreases.
    
- Statin plasma concentration increases.
    
- Risk of myopathy increases.
    

---

## 3. Organic Anion Transporters (OAT1, OAT3)

### Function

- Renal tubular secretion of acidic drugs
    

### Interaction

Inhibition of OAT transporters:

- Renal excretion decreases
    
- Drug accumulation occurs
    

### Example

**Probenecid + Penicillin**

- Probenecid inhibits OAT.
    
- Penicillin secretion decreases.
    
- Penicillin plasma level increases.
    

---

## 4. Organic Cation Transporters (OCT)

### Function

- Transport basic drugs in kidney and liver
    

### Interaction

Inhibition of OCT:

- Reduced drug elimination
    
- Increased plasma concentration
    

### Example

**Cimetidine + Metformin**

- Cimetidine inhibits OCT.
    
- Metformin clearance decreases.
    
- Metformin concentration increases.
    

---

## 5. Multidrug Resistance Proteins (MRP)

### Function

- Efflux transporters
    
- Remove drugs and metabolites from cells
    

### Interaction

Inhibition of MRP:

- Drug accumulation inside cells
    
- Increased toxicity
    

### Example

- Anticancer drug interactions involving MRP transporters.

# Clinical Significance

1. Increased drug toxicity.
    
2. Altered bioavailability.
    
3. Altered renal elimination.
    
4. Important cause of drug-drug interactions.
    
5. Requires dose adjustment and monitoring.
    

---

# Conclusion

Transporter-mediated drug interactions occur when drugs affect membrane transport proteins such as **P-glycoprotein (P-gp), OATP, OAT, OCT, and MRP**. These interactions alter drug absorption, distribution, and excretion, leading to changes in efficacy and toxicity. Clinically important examples include **Digoxin–Quinidine, Statin–Cyclosporine, Probenecid–Penicillin, and Metformin–Cimetidine** interactions.
