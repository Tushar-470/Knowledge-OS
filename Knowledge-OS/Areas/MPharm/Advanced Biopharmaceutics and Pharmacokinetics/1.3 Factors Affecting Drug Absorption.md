# Factors Affecting Drug Absorption (10 Marks)

## Definition

Drug absorption is influenced by various **physicochemical, physiological, and pharmaceutical factors**. These factors determine the rate and extent of drug absorption and ultimately affect bioavailability.

# I. Physicochemical (Drug-Related) Factors

## 1. Lipid Solubility

- Lipid-soluble drugs cross biological membranes easily.
    
- Higher lipid solubility → Greater absorption.
    

### Example

- Diazepam > Atenolol
    

---

## 2. Degree of Ionization (pKa)

- Unionized drugs are absorbed better than ionized drugs.
    
- Absorption depends on pH and pKa.
    

### Example

- Aspirin absorbed in stomach.
    
- Weak bases absorbed mainly in intestine.
    

---

## 3. Molecular Size

- Small molecules are absorbed more easily.
    
- Large molecules show poor absorption.
    

### Example

- Insulin is not absorbed orally.
    

---

## 4. Drug Solubility and Dissolution Rate

- Drug must dissolve before absorption.
    
- Faster dissolution → Faster absorption.
    

### Noyes-Whitney Equation

[  
\frac{dC}{dt}=\frac{DA(Cs-C)}{hV}  
]

---

## 5. Particle Size

- Smaller particle size increases surface area.
    
- Increases dissolution and absorption.
    

---

# II. Physiological Factors

## 1. Surface Area Available for Absorption

- Larger surface area → Greater absorption.
    
- Small intestine has villi and microvilli.
    

### Important Point

**Jejunum is the major site of absorption.**

---

## 2. Gastric Emptying Time

- Faster gastric emptying → Faster absorption.
    
- Fatty food delays gastric emptying.
    

---

## 3. Intestinal Transit Time

- Adequate contact time improves absorption.
    
- Diarrhea decreases absorption.
    

---

## 4. Blood Flow

- Higher blood flow maintains concentration gradient.
    
- Increases absorption.
    

---

## 5. Gastrointestinal pH

- Affects ionization and solubility.
    
- Stomach pH = acidic.
    
- Intestinal pH = alkaline.
    

---

## 6. First-Pass Metabolism

- Drug metabolized in intestine/liver before reaching circulation.
    
- Reduces bioavailability.
    

### Examples

- Propranolol
    
- Nitroglycerin
    
- Morphine
    

---

# III. Pharmaceutical Factors

## 1. Dosage Form

Rate of absorption:

```text
Solution > Suspension > Capsule > Tablet
```

---

## 2. Excipients

- Disintegrants enhance absorption.
    
- Lubricants may reduce dissolution.
    

---

## 3. Manufacturing Method

- Compression force
    
- Granulation method
    
- Coating
    

All affect dissolution and absorption.

# Conclusion

Drug absorption depends on the interaction of **drug-related factors, physiological conditions of the GIT, and pharmaceutical formulation factors**. Among physiological factors, the **large surface area of the small intestine** makes it the principal site of absorption. Proper understanding of these factors is essential for optimizing bioavailability.
