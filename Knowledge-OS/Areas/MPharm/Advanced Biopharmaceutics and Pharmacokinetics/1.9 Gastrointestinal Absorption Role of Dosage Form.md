# Gastrointestinal Absorption: Role of Dosage Form

## Introduction

The dosage form influences the rate and extent of drug absorption from the gastrointestinal tract. Before absorption, the drug must be released from the dosage form and dissolved in GI fluids. Generally:

```text
Solution > Suspension > Capsule > Tablet
```

in terms of absorption rate.

---

# 1. Solution as a Dosage Form

### Types

- Solution
    
- Syrup
    
- Elixir
    

### Definition

Drug is completely dissolved in the solvent before administration.

### Absorption Characteristics

- No disintegration required.
    
- No dissolution required.
    
- Drug is already in dissolved form.
    
- Fastest absorption.
    
- Rapid onset of action.
    

### Advantages

- High bioavailability.
    
- Uniform drug distribution.
    
- Suitable for pediatric and geriatric patients.
    

### Examples

- Paracetamol syrup
    
- Cough syrups
    
- Elixirs
    

### Diagram

```text
Solution
   ↓
Direct Absorption
   ↓
Blood
```

---

# 2. Suspension as a Dosage Form

### Definition

A suspension contains finely divided insoluble drug particles dispersed in a liquid medium.

### Absorption Characteristics

- No disintegration needed.
    
- Drug must dissolve before absorption.
    
- Faster absorption than tablets and capsules.
    
- Slower than solutions.
    

### Advantages

- Suitable for poorly soluble drugs.
    
- Improved stability.
    

### Factors Affecting Absorption

- Particle size
    
- Wettability
    
- Viscosity
    

### Example

- Antacid suspensions
    
- Amoxicillin suspension
    

### Diagram

```text
Suspension
     ↓
Drug Particles
     ↓
Dissolution
     ↓
Absorption
```

---

# 3. Capsule as a Dosage Form

### Definition

A capsule is a solid dosage form in which the drug is enclosed in a hard or soft gelatin shell.

### Absorption Characteristics

- Capsule shell dissolves first.
    
- Drug released into GI fluid.
    
- Drug dissolves and is absorbed.
    

### Advantages

- Faster absorption than tablets.
    
- Better patient acceptance.
    
- Masks unpleasant taste.
    

### Example

- Omeprazole capsules
    
- Doxycycline capsules
    

### Diagram

```text
Capsule
    ↓
Shell Dissolution
    ↓
Drug Release
    ↓
Dissolution
    ↓
Absorption
```

---

# 4. Tablet as a Dosage Form

### Definition

A tablet is a compressed solid dosage form containing drug and excipients.

### Absorption Characteristics

Tablet undergoes:

```text
Tablet
   ↓
Disintegration
   ↓
Granules
   ↓
Deaggregation
   ↓
Drug Particles
   ↓
Dissolution
   ↓
Absorption
```

### Characteristics

- Slowest among conventional dosage forms.
    
- Multiple steps before absorption.
    
- Dissolution often becomes rate-limiting.
    

### Advantages

- Stable
    
- Convenient
    
- Economical
    

### Example

- Paracetamol tablet
    
- Metformin tablet

# Comparative Table

|Dosage Form|Disintegration|Dissolution|Absorption Rate|
|---|---|---|---|
|Solution|Not Required|Not Required|Fastest|
|Suspension|Not Required|Required|Fast|
|Capsule|Required (Shell)|Required|Moderate|
|Tablet|Required|Required|Slowest|

# Conclusion

The dosage form plays a critical role in gastrointestinal absorption. Solutions provide the fastest absorption because the drug is already dissolved, whereas tablets show the slowest absorption due to the additional steps of disintegration and dissolution. Therefore:

### Order of Absorption

✅ **Solution > Suspension > Capsule > Tablet**

# 1-Minute Revision

### Remember This Order

```text
Solution > Suspension > Capsule > Tablet
```

### Why?

- Solution → Already dissolved
    
- Suspension → Needs dissolution
    
- Capsule → Shell dissolution + drug dissolution
    
- Tablet → Disintegration + dissolution
    

### Most Important Exam Line

**"The more steps required before dissolution, the slower the gastrointestinal absorption."** ✅
