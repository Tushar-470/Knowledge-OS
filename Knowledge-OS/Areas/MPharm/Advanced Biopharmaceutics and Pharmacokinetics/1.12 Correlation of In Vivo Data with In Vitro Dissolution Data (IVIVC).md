# Correlation of In Vivo Data with In Vitro Dissolution Data (IVIVC) (10 Marks)

## Definition

**In Vitro–In Vivo Correlation (IVIVC)** is a predictive mathematical relationship between:

- **In vitro dissolution rate** (drug release in laboratory)
    
- **In vivo absorption/bioavailability** (drug absorption in the body)
    

It helps predict the in vivo performance of a dosage form from dissolution test results.

---

# Need for IVIVC

1. Predict bioavailability from dissolution data.
    
2. Reduce human bioavailability studies.
    
3. Support formulation development.
    
4. Assist in regulatory approval.
    
5. Ensure batch-to-batch consistency.
    

---

# Principle

```text
In Vitro Dissolution
          ↓
 Mathematical Correlation
          ↓
In Vivo Absorption
          ↓
Plasma Drug Concentration
```

The better the dissolution, the better the absorption (for many drugs).

---

# Levels of IVIVC

## 1. Level A Correlation ⭐ (Most Important)

### Definition

A **point-to-point relationship** between in vitro dissolution and in vivo drug absorption.

### Characteristics

- Highest level correlation.
    
- Most informative.
    
- Predicts complete plasma concentration profile.
    

### Representation

```text
% Drug Dissolved
        ↔
% Drug Absorbed
```

### Advantage

- Can replace certain bioequivalence studies.
    

---

## 2. Level B Correlation

### Definition

Relationship between statistical moments of in vitro and in vivo data.

### Examples

- Mean Dissolution Time (MDT)
    
- Mean Residence Time (MRT)
    

### Limitation

- Not a point-to-point correlation.
    
- Less predictive than Level A.
    

---

## 3. Level C Correlation

### Definition

Relationship between one dissolution parameter and one pharmacokinetic parameter.

### Examples

- % dissolved at 4 hr vs Cmax
    
- % dissolved at 6 hr vs AUC
    

### Limitation

- Weakest correlation.

# Advantages of IVIVC

1. Reduces number of human studies.
    
2. Saves time and cost.
    
3. Helps formulation optimization.
    
4. Supports regulatory changes.
    
5. Predicts product performance.
    

---

# Limitations

1. Not possible for all drugs.
    
2. Difficult for drugs with complex absorption.
    
3. Requires accurate dissolution data.
    
4. Poor correlation for some immediate-release products.
    

---

# Conclusion

**IVIVC** establishes a relationship between **in vitro dissolution** and **in vivo absorption**. Among all levels, **Level A correlation** is the most important because it provides a direct point-to-point relationship between drug dissolution and absorption and is widely accepted by regulatory agencies.
