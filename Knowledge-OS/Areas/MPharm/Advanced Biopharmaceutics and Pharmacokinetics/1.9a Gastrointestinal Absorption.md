# Gastrointestinal Absorption (10 Marks)

## Definition

**Gastrointestinal absorption** is the process by which a drug moves from the gastrointestinal tract (GIT) into the systemic circulation after oral administration. It is the most common route of drug absorption and determines the bioavailability of orally administered drugs.

---

# Process of Gastrointestinal Absorption

```text
Oral Drug
    ↓
Disintegration
    ↓
Dissolution
    ↓
Drug in Solution
    ↓
Absorption Across GIT Membrane
    ↓
Portal Blood
    ↓
Liver (First-Pass Effect)
    ↓
Systemic Circulation
```

---

# Sites of Absorption in GIT

|Region|pH|Absorption|
|---|---|---|
|Stomach|1–3|Limited absorption|
|Duodenum|5–6.5|Rapid absorption begins|
|Jejunum|6.5–7.5|**Major absorption site**|
|Ileum|7–8|Secondary absorption|
|Colon|6–7.5|Minimal absorption|

---

# Why is the Small Intestine the Main Absorption Site?

### 1. Large Surface Area

- Presence of folds, villi, and microvilli.
    
- Surface area up to 200 m².
    

### 2. Rich Blood Supply

- Maintains concentration gradient.
    

### 3. Long Contact Time

- Drug remains in intestine for several hours.
    

### 4. Favorable pH

- Suitable for absorption of most drugs.
    

---

# Mechanisms of Gastrointestinal Absorption

## 1. Passive Diffusion (Most Common)

- No energy required.
    
- Drug moves from high concentration to low concentration.
    

### Example

- Aspirin
    
- Paracetamol
    

---

## 2. Active Transport

- Energy-dependent.
    
- Carrier-mediated.
    

### Example

- Levodopa
    
- Iron
    

---

## 3. Facilitated Diffusion

- Carrier-mediated.
    
- No energy required.
    

---

## 4. Endocytosis

- Used for large molecules.
    

### Example

- Vitamin B12–Intrinsic Factor complex
    

---

# Factors Affecting Gastrointestinal Absorption

## Physiological Factors

- Gastric emptying rate
    
- Intestinal motility
    
- Blood flow
    
- GI pH
    
- First-pass metabolism
    

## Drug Factors

- Lipid solubility
    
- pKa
    
- Particle size
    
- Solubility
    

## Formulation Factors

- Dosage form
    
- Excipients
    
- Manufacturing process
    

---

# First-Pass Effect

After absorption, drugs enter the portal vein and pass through the liver.

Some drugs undergo metabolism before reaching systemic circulation.

### Examples

- Propranolol
    
- Morphine
    
- Nitroglycerin
    

Result:

✅ Bioavailability decreases

# Conclusion

Gastrointestinal absorption is the transfer of drug from the GIT into systemic circulation. The **small intestine, particularly the jejunum, is the major site of absorption** because of its large surface area, rich blood supply, and favorable physiological conditions. Drug absorption is influenced by drug properties, GI physiology, and formulation factors.
