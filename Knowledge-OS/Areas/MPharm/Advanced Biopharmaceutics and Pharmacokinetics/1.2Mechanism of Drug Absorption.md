# Mechanism of Drug Absorption (10 Marks)

## Definition

**Drug absorption** is the process by which a drug moves from its site of administration into the systemic circulation. For orally administered drugs, absorption mainly occurs through the gastrointestinal membrane.

---

# Mechanisms of Drug Absorption

There are **6 main mechanisms** of drug absorption.

---

# 1. Passive Diffusion (Most Common)

### Definition

Movement of drug from higher concentration to lower concentration without energy expenditure.

### Characteristics

- No carrier required.
    
- No energy required.
    
- Follows concentration gradient.
    
- Most drugs are absorbed by this mechanism.
    

### Examples

- Aspirin
    
- Diazepam
    
- Paracetamol
    

```text
High Concentration  →→→  Low Concentration
```

---

# 2. Facilitated Diffusion

### Definition

Drug moves from higher concentration to lower concentration with the help of a carrier protein.

### Characteristics

- Carrier required.
    
- No energy required.
    
- Saturable process.
    

### Examples

- Vitamin B12
    
- Some sugars
    

```text
Drug → Carrier → Cell
```

---

# 3. Active Transport

### Definition

Drug is transported against the concentration gradient using a carrier and energy (ATP).

### Characteristics

- Carrier required.
    
- Energy required.
    
- Saturable and selective.
    

### Examples

- Levodopa
    
- Methyldopa
    
- Iron
    

```text
Low Conc. → Carrier + ATP → High Conc.
```

---

# 4. Endocytosis (Pinocytosis)

### Definition

Cell membrane engulfs drug particles forming vesicles.

### Characteristics

- Used for large molecules.
    
- Energy dependent.
    

### Examples

- Vitamin B12–Intrinsic factor complex
    
- Proteins and peptides
    

```text
Drug
 ↓
Cell membrane surrounds
 ↓
Vesicle formed
 ↓
Drug enters cell
```

---

# 5. Paracellular Transport

### Definition

Drug passes through aqueous pores or tight junctions between adjacent cells.

### Characteristics

- Important for small hydrophilic molecules.
    
- Limited absorption.
    

### Examples

- Mannitol
    
- Atenolol
    

---

# 6. Efflux Transport (P-gp)

### Definition

Transport proteins pump absorbed drug back into the intestinal lumen.

### Characteristics

- Reduces absorption.
    
- Important cause of low bioavailability.
    

### Example

- Digoxin
    
- Cyclosporine
    

```text
Drug enters cell
      ↓
P-gp pump
      ↓
Drug pumped back
```

# Factors Favoring Absorption

1. High lipid solubility
    
2. Unionized form of drug
    
3. Large surface area
    
4. Good blood supply
    
5. Longer contact time
    

---

# Conclusion

Drug absorption occurs by several mechanisms, but **passive diffusion is the most important and most common mechanism**. Active transport, facilitated diffusion, endocytosis, paracellular transport, and efflux transport also contribute to drug absorption depending on the physicochemical properties of the drug.
