# Factors Affecting the Dissolution Rate (10 Marks)

## Definition

The **dissolution rate** is the amount of drug dissolved per unit time under specified conditions. According to the **Noyes–Whitney equation**, dissolution rate depends on several physicochemical and formulation factors.

---

# Noyes–Whitney Equation

# [  
\frac{dC}{dt}

\frac{D \times A \times (C_s-C)}  
{h \times V}  
]

Where:

- D = Diffusion coefficient
    
- A = Surface area
    
- Cs = Saturation solubility
    
- C = Drug concentration
    
- h = Diffusion layer thickness
    
- V = Volume of medium
    

---

# Factors Affecting Dissolution Rate

## 1. Surface Area (A)

- Smaller particle size → Larger surface area.
    
- Larger surface area → Faster dissolution.
    

### Example

- Micronized Griseofulvin
    

✅ Surface area ↑ → Dissolution ↑

---

## 2. Solubility of Drug (Cs)

- Higher solubility increases concentration gradient.
    
- Faster dissolution occurs.
    

### Methods to Increase Solubility

- Salt formation
    
- pH adjustment
    
- Cosolvents
    

✅ Solubility ↑ → Dissolution ↑

---

## 3. Diffusion Coefficient (D)

- Indicates speed of drug movement in solution.
    
- Increases with temperature.
    

### Stokes-Einstein Relationship

[  
D \propto \frac{T}{\eta}  
]

Where:

- T = Temperature
    
- η = Viscosity
    

✅ D ↑ → Dissolution ↑

---

## 4. Diffusion Layer Thickness (h)

- Thin diffusion layer promotes faster diffusion.
    
- Agitation reduces h.
    

### Example

- Stirring during dissolution test
    

✅ h ↓ → Dissolution ↑

---

## 5. Concentration Gradient (Cs − C)

- Larger difference between surface concentration and bulk concentration increases dissolution.
    

### Sink Condition

[  
C << Cs  
]

Maximum dissolution occurs under sink conditions.

✅ (Cs − C) ↑ → Dissolution ↑

---

## 6. Temperature

- Higher temperature increases:
    
    - Solubility
        
    - Diffusion coefficient
        

### Result

- Faster dissolution
    

✅ Temperature ↑ → Dissolution ↑

---

## 7. Agitation/Stirring

- Reduces diffusion layer thickness.
    
- Maintains sink condition.
    

### Example

- USP Dissolution Apparatus
    

✅ Agitation ↑ → Dissolution ↑

---

## 8. Crystal Form (Polymorphism)

### Amorphous Form

- Higher energy
    
- Faster dissolution
    

### Crystalline Form

- Lower energy
    
- Slower dissolution
    

### Order

```text
Amorphous > Metastable Crystal > Stable Crystal
```

✅ Amorphous form dissolves faster.

---

## 9. Wettability

- Better wetting increases contact between drug and solvent.
    
- Surfactants improve wettability.
    

### Example

- Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS)
    

✅ Wettability ↑ → Dissolution ↑

---

## 10. Formulation Factors

### Increase Dissolution

- Hydrophilic diluents
    
- Superdisintegrants
    
- Surfactants
    

### Decrease Dissolution

- Excess magnesium stearate
    
- Hydrophobic excipients

# Conclusion

The dissolution rate of a drug is governed by **surface area, solubility, diffusion coefficient, diffusion layer thickness, concentration gradient, temperature, agitation, crystal form, wettability, and formulation factors**. Among these, **particle size (surface area)** and **solubility** are the most important factors in formulation development.
