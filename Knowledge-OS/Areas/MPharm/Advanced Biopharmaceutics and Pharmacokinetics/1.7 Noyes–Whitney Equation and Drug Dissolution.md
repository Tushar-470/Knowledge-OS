# Noyes–Whitney Equation and Drug Dissolution (10 Marks)

## Definition

The **Noyes–Whitney equation** describes the rate at which a solid drug dissolves in a dissolution medium. It is the fundamental equation used to explain **drug dissolution** and predict bioavailability of poorly soluble drugs.

---

# Historical Background

- **1897** – Noyes and Whitney proposed the dissolution concept.
    
- Later, **Nernst and Brunner** gave the diffusion layer theory and modern form of the equation.
    

---

# Noyes–Whitney Equation

# [  
\frac{dC}{dt}

\frac{D \times A \times (C_s-C)}  
{h \times V}  
]

Where:

|Symbol|Meaning|
|---|---|
|dC/dt|Dissolution rate|
|D|Diffusion coefficient|
|A|Surface area of drug|
|Cs|Saturation solubility|
|C|Drug concentration in bulk solution|
|h|Thickness of diffusion layer|
|V|Volume of dissolution medium|

---

# Diffusion Layer Model

```text
      Solid Drug

     Cs (Saturated Layer)
 ─────────────────────────
      Diffusion Layer (h)
 ─────────────────────────
      Bulk Solution (C)
```

- Drug dissolves at surface (Cs).
    
- Diffuses through diffusion layer (h).
    
- Reaches bulk solution (C).
    

---

# Derivation (Short Exam Version)

According to Fick's First Law:

[  
J=-D\frac{dC}{dx}  
]

For a linear concentration gradient:

[  
J=D\frac{(C_s-C)}{h}  
]

Since,

[  
Rate = J \times A  
]

Therefore,

# [  
\frac{dM}{dt}

\frac{DA(C_s-C)}{h}  
]

Dividing by volume (V):

# [  
\frac{dC}{dt}

\frac{DA(C_s-C)}  
{hV}  
]

This is the **Noyes–Whitney equation**.

---

# Factors Affecting Drug Dissolution

## Increase Dissolution Rate

### 1. Surface Area (A) ↑

- Smaller particle size
    
- Micronization
    

### 2. Solubility (Cs) ↑

- Salt formation
    
- pH adjustment
    

### 3. Diffusion Coefficient (D) ↑

- Higher temperature
    

### 4. Agitation ↑

- Decreases diffusion layer thickness (h)
    

---

## Decrease Dissolution Rate

### 1. Diffusion Layer Thickness (h) ↑

- Less stirring
    

### 2. Hydrophobic Excipients

- Poor wetting
    

---

# Importance in Drug Dissolution

1. Explains dissolution mechanism.
    
2. Predicts absorption of poorly soluble drugs.
    
3. Helps improve bioavailability.
    
4. Basis for dissolution testing.
    
5. Important for BCS Class II drugs.

# Conclusion

The **Noyes–Whitney equation**

# [  
\frac{dC}{dt}

\frac{DA(C_s-C)}  
{hV}  
]

is the fundamental equation describing drug dissolution. It shows that dissolution rate increases with **surface area, solubility, and diffusion coefficient**, and decreases with **diffusion layer thickness**. It is widely used in formulation development and bioavailability studies.
