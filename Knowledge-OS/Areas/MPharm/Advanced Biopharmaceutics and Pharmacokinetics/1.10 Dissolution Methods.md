# Dissolution Methods (10 Marks)

## Definition

**Dissolution methods** are standardized procedures used to determine the rate and extent of drug dissolution from a dosage form into a dissolution medium under specified conditions. They are used for quality control, formulation development, and bioavailability prediction.

---

# Objectives of Dissolution Testing

1. Evaluate drug release from dosage forms.
    
2. Compare different formulations.
    
3. Ensure batch-to-batch consistency.
    
4. Predict in vivo performance.
    
5. Support bioavailability and bioequivalence studies.
    

---

# Official USP Dissolution Methods

## 1. USP Apparatus I (Rotating Basket Method)

### Principle

The dosage form is placed in a wire mesh basket rotating in dissolution medium.

### Diagram

```text
 Motor
   │
Rotating Basket
   │
 Tablet/Capsule
   │
Dissolution Medium
```

### Conditions

- Basket speed: 25–150 rpm
    
- Temperature: 37 ± 0.5°C
    

### Uses

- Capsules
    
- Floating dosage forms
    
- Tablets that tend to float
    

### Advantages

- Suitable for floating formulations.
    

---

## 2. USP Apparatus II (Paddle Method)

### Principle

A paddle rotates above the dosage form placed at the bottom of the vessel.

### Diagram

```text
 Motor
   │
 Paddle
   │
────────────
 Tablet
────────────
Dissolution Medium
```

### Conditions

- Paddle speed: 25–100 rpm
    
- Temperature: 37 ± 0.5°C
    

### Uses

- Tablets
    
- Capsules
    
- Most commonly used method
    

### Advantages

- Simple
    
- Reproducible
    
- Widely accepted
    

---

## 3. USP Apparatus III (Reciprocating Cylinder)

### Principle

Cylinder moves up and down in dissolution medium.

### Uses

- Modified-release formulations
    
- Controlled-release products
    

---

## 4. USP Apparatus IV (Flow-Through Cell)

### Principle

Fresh dissolution medium continuously flows through the cell containing the dosage form.

### Uses

- Poorly soluble drugs
    
- Extended-release formulations
    

---

## 5. USP Apparatus V (Paddle over Disc)

### Uses

- Transdermal patches
    

---

## 6. USP Apparatus VI (Rotating Cylinder)

### Uses

- Transdermal drug delivery systems
    

---

## 7. USP Apparatus VII (Reciprocating Holder)

### Uses

- Transdermal systems
    
- Modified-release dosage forms

# Factors Controlled During Dissolution Testing

- Temperature (37 ± 0.5°C)
    
- pH of medium
    
- Agitation speed
    
- Volume of medium
    
- Sampling time
    

---

# Importance

1. Quality control test.
    
2. Detect formulation changes.
    
3. Predict bioavailability.
    
4. Support IVIVC studies.
    
5. Regulatory requirement.

# Conclusion

Dissolution methods are essential tools for evaluating drug release from dosage forms. Among all methods, **USP Apparatus I (Basket)** and **USP Apparatus II (Paddle)** are the most commonly used for routine dissolution testing of oral solid dosage forms.
