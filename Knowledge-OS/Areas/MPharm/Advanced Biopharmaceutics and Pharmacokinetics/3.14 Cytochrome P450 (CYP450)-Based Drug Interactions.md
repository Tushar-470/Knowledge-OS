# Cytochrome P450 (CYP450)-Based Drug Interactions (10 Marks)

## Introduction

The **Cytochrome P450 (CYP450)** enzyme system is a group of hepatic and intestinal enzymes responsible for the metabolism of about **75% of clinically used drugs**. Drug interactions occur when one drug inhibits or induces these enzymes, thereby altering the metabolism of another drug.

---

# What is CYP450?

CYP450 enzymes are heme-containing monooxygenase enzymes mainly present in:

- Liver
    
- Intestinal mucosa
    

### Important Isoenzymes

- CYP3A4 (Most important)
    
- CYP2D6
    
- CYP2C9
    
- CYP2C19
    
- CYP1A2
    

---

# Types of CYP450-Based Drug Interactions

## 1. Enzyme Inhibition

### Definition

One drug inhibits CYP450 enzymes and decreases metabolism of another drug.

### Mechanism

```text
Perpetrator Drug
        ↓
Inhibits CYP450
        ↓
Metabolism Decreases
        ↓
Plasma Drug Level Increases
        ↓
Toxicity Increases
```

### Effects

- Clearance ↓
    
- Half-life ↑
    
- AUC ↑
    
- Drug accumulation
    

### Examples

|Inhibitor|Victim Drug|Effect|
|---|---|---|
|Erythromycin|Theophylline|Toxicity|
|Ketoconazole|Cyclosporine|Increased levels|
|Clarithromycin|Statins|Myopathy|
|Ritonavir|Many antivirals|Increased concentration|

---

## 2. Enzyme Induction

### Definition

One drug increases synthesis of CYP450 enzymes and accelerates metabolism of another drug.

### Mechanism

```text
Perpetrator Drug
        ↓
Induces CYP450
        ↓
Metabolism Increases
        ↓
Plasma Drug Level Decreases
        ↓
Therapeutic Failure
```

### Effects

- Clearance ↑
    
- Half-life ↓
    
- AUC ↓
    
- Reduced efficacy
    

### Examples

|Inducer|Victim Drug|Effect|
|---|---|---|
|Rifampicin|Warfarin|Reduced anticoagulation|
|Carbamazepine|Oral contraceptives|Contraceptive failure|
|Phenytoin|Many drugs|Reduced efficacy|
|Phenobarbital|Various drugs|Increased metabolism|

# Major CYP Isoenzymes and Examples

|CYP Enzyme|Common Substrates|Inhibitors|Inducers|
|---|---|---|---|
|CYP3A4|Cyclosporine, Statins, Midazolam|Ketoconazole, Clarithromycin|Rifampicin|
|CYP2C9|Warfarin, Phenytoin|Fluconazole|Rifampicin|
|CYP2D6|Codeine, Metoprolol|Quinidine|Few important inducers|
|CYP1A2|Theophylline|Ciprofloxacin|Smoking|
|CYP2C19|Omeprazole|Fluoxetine|Rifampicin|

# Clinical Significance

### 1. Increased Toxicity

Example:

- Erythromycin + Theophylline
    
- Increased theophylline concentration
    

### 2. Therapeutic Failure

Example:

- Rifampicin + Oral contraceptives
    
- Reduced contraceptive effect
    

### 3. Dose Adjustment Required

- Warfarin
    
- Phenytoin
    
- Cyclosporine
    
- Digoxin

# Conclusion

Cytochrome P450-based drug interactions are among the most clinically important pharmacokinetic interactions. They occur through **enzyme inhibition** or **enzyme induction**, leading to changes in drug metabolism, plasma concentration, efficacy, and toxicity. CYP3A4 is the most important enzyme involved in these interactions.
