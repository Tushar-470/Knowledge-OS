
# Basic Considerations in Pharmacokinetics (10 Marks)

## Definition

**Pharmacokinetics (PK)** is the study of **what the body does to the drug**. It describes the movement of a drug in the body through **Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, and Excretion (ADME).**

---

# ADME Processes

### 1. Absorption

Movement of drug from the site of administration into the bloodstream.

### 2. Distribution

Movement of drug from blood to different tissues and organs.

### 3. Metabolism

Conversion of drug into metabolites, mainly in the liver.

### 4. Excretion

Removal of drug and metabolites from the body through urine, bile, etc.

**Mnemonic:** **A D M E**

---

# Fundamental Pharmacokinetic Parameters

## 1. Volume of Distribution (Vd)

Definition:  
Apparent volume in which the drug is distributed in the body.

### Formula:

$$
V_d = \frac{\text{Dose}}{C_0}
$$

Where:

- Vd = Volume of distribution
    
- C₀ = Initial plasma concentration
    

### Significance:

- High Vd → extensive tissue distribution
    
- Low Vd → drug remains mainly in plasma
    

---

## 2. Clearance (CL)

Definition:  
Volume of plasma cleared of drug per unit time.

### Formula:

$$
CL = \frac{\text{Dose}}{AUC}
$$


### Significance:

- Indicates efficiency of drug elimination.
    
- Most important PK parameter.
    

---

## 3. Half-Life (t½)

Definition:  
Time required for plasma drug concentration to decrease by 50%.

### Formula:

$$
t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{K_{el}}
$$


### Significance:

- Determines dosing interval.
    
- Determines time to reach steady state.
    

---

## 4. Area Under Curve (AUC)

Definition:  
Total drug exposure in the body.

### Formula:

$$
AUC = \frac{\text{Dose}}{CL}
$$


### Significance:

- Used in bioavailability and bioequivalence studies.
    

---

# Rate Processes

## Zero-Order Kinetics

- Drug eliminated at a constant rate.
    
- Independent of concentration.
    
- Example: Ethanol.
    

### Equation:

$$
\frac{dC}{dt} = -k_0
$$


## First-Order Kinetics

- Drug elimination proportional to concentration.
    
- Most drugs follow first-order kinetics.
    

### Equation:

$$
\frac{dC}{dt} = -kC
$$


---

# Plasma Protein Binding

Only free (unbound) drug is active.

### Formula:
$$
f_u = \frac{\text{Free Drug}}{\text{Total Drug}}
$$


Effects:

- Increased protein binding → decreased Vd.
    
- Affects clearance and drug action.
    

---

# Conclusion

Pharmacokinetics is the quantitative study of **ADME** processes. Important parameters include **Vd, CL, t½, and AUC**, which help in designing safe and effective dosage regimens.
