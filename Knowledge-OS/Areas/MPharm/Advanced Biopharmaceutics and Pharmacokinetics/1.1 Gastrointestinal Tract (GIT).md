# Gastrointestinal Tract (GIT) – 10 Marks (Easy Exam Language)

## Definition

The **Gastrointestinal Tract (GIT)** is a long muscular tube extending from the **mouth to the anus**, about **9 meters long** in adults. It is the main site for digestion, absorption of nutrients, and absorption of orally administered drugs.

# Regions of GIT and Their Importance

|Region|pH|Importance|
|---|---|---|
|Stomach|1.2–3.5|Limited absorption, weak acids absorbed|
|Duodenum|5–6.5|Rapid absorption begins|
|Jejunum|6.5–7.5|**Main absorption site**|
|Ileum|7–8|Secondary absorption|
|Colon|6–7.5|Minimal absorption|
|Rectum|7.2–7.4|Bypasses first-pass metabolism|

---

# Physiological Features Affecting Drug Absorption

## 1. Villi and Microvilli

- Present in small intestine.
    
- Increase surface area greatly.
    
- Help in maximum drug absorption.
    

**Therefore, most drugs are absorbed in the small intestine.**

---

## 2. Gastric Emptying

- Movement of drug from stomach to intestine.
    
- Faster emptying → Faster absorption.
    
- Fatty food delays gastric emptying.
    

---

## 3. Intestinal Motility

- Peristaltic movement moves drug through intestine.
    
- Excessive motility (diarrhea) decreases absorption.
    

---

## 4. Blood Flow

- High blood flow maintains concentration gradient.
    
- Enhances drug absorption.
    

---

## 5. First-Pass Metabolism

- Drugs absorbed from intestine pass through liver first.
    
- Some drug is metabolized before reaching systemic circulation.
    
- Bioavailability decreases.
    

### Examples

- Propranolol
    
- Morphine
    
- Nitroglycerin
    

---

# Clinical Significance

- GIT is the major site of oral drug absorption.
    
- pH, surface area, and transit time affect bioavailability.
    
- Jejunum is the most important site for drug absorption.
    

---

# Conclusion

The GIT is the principal organ responsible for oral drug absorption. Among all regions, the **small intestine (especially jejunum)** is the major absorption site because of its large surface area, villi, microvilli, and rich blood supply.
