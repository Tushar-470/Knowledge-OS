# PHYTOSOMES – Preparation and Applications (10 Marks)

## Definition

Phytosomes are advanced herbal drug delivery systems in which a **phytoconstituent (plant extract/active phytochemical) forms a molecular complex with phosphatidylcholine**, resulting in improved absorption and bioavailability. Unlike liposomes, the phytochemical is chemically complexed with phospholipid rather than simply entrapped.

---

# Preparation of Phytosomes

Phytosomes are generally prepared by **complexation of phytoconstituents with phosphatidylcholine (PC)** in a suitable solvent. The most common methods are:

## 1. Solvent Evaporation Method (Most Common)

### Procedure

1. Weigh phytochemical (e.g., curcumin, silymarin, quercetin) and phosphatidylcholine in a suitable molar ratio (usually 1:1 or 1:2).
    
2. Dissolve both in anhydrous organic solvent such as ethanol, methanol, or acetone.
    
3. Reflux the mixture at 40–60°C with continuous stirring for 2–4 hours.
    
4. Hydrogen bonding occurs between phytochemical and phosphatidylcholine forming a molecular complex.
    
5. Remove solvent using rotary evaporation under reduced pressure.
    
6. Dry the residue under vacuum.
    
7. Collect and store the phytosome powder.
    

### Flow Chart

```text
Phytochemical + Phosphatidylcholine
                 ↓
 Dissolution in Organic Solvent
                 ↓
      Reflux and Stirring
                 ↓
      Complex Formation
                 ↓
      Solvent Evaporation
                 ↓
         Drying
                 ↓
         PHYTOSOMES
```

---

## 2. Antisolvent Precipitation Method

### Procedure

1. Dissolve phosphatidylcholine and phytochemical in ethanol/methanol.
    
2. Add the solution into excess cold water with vigorous stirring.
    
3. Phytosome complex precipitates out.
    
4. Collect by filtration or centrifugation.
    
5. Dry the precipitate.
    

---

## 3. Supercritical Fluid Technology

### Procedure

1. Dissolve phytochemical and phosphatidylcholine in supercritical CO₂.
    
2. Rapid pressure reduction causes precipitation of phytosome nanoparticles.
    
3. Collect and dry the particles.
    

### Advantages

- Uniform particle size
    
- No solvent residue
    
- Suitable for heat-sensitive compounds

# Applications of Phytosomes

## 1. Hepatoprotective Drug Delivery

Used for delivery of **Silymarin Phytosome (Siliphos®)**.

### Benefits

- Improved liver protection
    
- Enhanced bioavailability
    
- Used in liver cirrhosis, hepatitis, and fatty liver disease
    

---

## 2. Anti-inflammatory Therapy

Used for **Curcumin Phytosome (Meriva®)**.

### Benefits

- Better absorption of curcumin
    
- Management of arthritis
    
- Reduction of inflammation
    

---

## 3. Antioxidant Delivery

Used for:

- Grape seed extract phytosomes
    
- Green tea phytosomes
    

### Benefits

- Improved antioxidant activity
    
- Protection against oxidative stress
    

---

## 4. Cardiovascular Protection

Used for:

- Grape seed phytosomes
    
- Green tea phytosomes
    

### Benefits

- Improved vascular function
    
- Protection from cardiovascular diseases
    

---

## 5. Neuroprotective Applications

Used for:

- Ginkgo biloba phytosome
    

### Benefits

- Improved cognitive function
    
- Better brain penetration
    
- Support in memory disorders
    

---

## 6. Wound Healing

Used for:

- Centella asiatica phytosome
    

### Benefits

- Enhanced wound healing
    
- Improved tissue regeneration
    

---

## 7. Cancer Therapy

Phytosomes improve delivery of phytochemicals such as:

- Curcumin
    
- Resveratrol
    
- Quercetin
    

### Benefits

- Enhanced cellular uptake
    
- Increased therapeutic efficacy
    

---

## 8. Oral Delivery of Herbal Drugs

### Benefits

- Increased absorption
    
- Increased bioavailability
    
- Reduced dose requirement
    
- Better patient compliance
    

---

# Advantages of Phytosomes

- Improved bioavailability
    
- Enhanced membrane permeability
    
- Better absorption of herbal drugs
    
- Improved therapeutic efficacy
    
- Reduced dose requirement
    
- Good biocompatibility
    
- Improved stability of phytoconstituents
    

---

# Conclusion

Phytosomes are phospholipid-based drug delivery systems prepared mainly by **solvent evaporation, antisolvent precipitation, and supercritical fluid techniques**. They enhance the bioavailability and therapeutic efficacy of phytoconstituents by forming a molecular complex with phosphatidylcholine. They are widely used for hepatoprotective, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, cardiovascular, neuroprotective, wound healing, and anticancer applications.
