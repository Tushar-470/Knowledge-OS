# MICROSPHERES

## Types, Preparation and Evaluation (10 Marks)

# Introduction

Microspheres are free-flowing spherical particles of size **1–1000 μm** in which the drug is uniformly dispersed throughout a polymer matrix. Unlike microcapsules, microspheres do not possess a distinct core and shell structure. Drug release occurs by diffusion, swelling, erosion, or degradation of the polymer matrix. They are widely used for controlled, sustained, and targeted drug delivery.

---

# Types of Microspheres

## 1. Biodegradable Polymeric Microspheres

Prepared using biodegradable polymers.

### Examples

- PLGA (Poly Lactic-co-Glycolic Acid)
    
- PLA (Poly Lactic Acid)
    
- PCL (Polycaprolactone)
    
- Chitosan
    
- Albumin
    
- Gelatin
    

### Advantages

- Biocompatible
    
- Biodegradable
    
- Sustained drug release
    

---

## 2. Non-Biodegradable Polymeric Microspheres

Prepared using insoluble polymers.

### Examples

- Ethyl Cellulose
    
- Eudragit RS/RL
    
- PMMA (Polymethyl Methacrylate)
    

### Application

- Controlled release formulations
    
- Oral sustained release systems
    

---

## 3. Bioadhesive Microspheres

### Characteristics

- Adhere to mucosal surfaces.
    
- Increase residence time.
    

### Examples

- Chitosan microspheres
    
- Carbopol microspheres
    

### Applications

- Nasal delivery
    
- Buccal delivery
    
- Vaginal delivery
    

---

## 4. Floating Microspheres

### Characteristics

- Low density
    
- Float on gastric fluid
    

### Applications

- Gastro-retentive drug delivery
    
- Prolonged gastric residence time
    

---

## 5. Magnetic Microspheres

### Characteristics

Contain magnetic material such as iron oxide.

### Application

- Targeted drug delivery using external magnetic field.
    

---

## 6. Radioactive Microspheres

### Characteristics

Contain radioactive isotopes.

### Application

- Cancer therapy
    
- Radioembolization
    

### Example

Yttrium-90 microspheres.

---

# Preparation of Microspheres

## 1. Solvent Evaporation Method

### Principle

Polymer and drug dissolved in volatile organic solvent.

### Procedure

1. Drug and polymer dissolved in solvent.
    
2. Emulsified in aqueous phase containing stabilizer.
    
3. Solvent evaporated.
    
4. Polymer precipitates forming microspheres.
    

### Advantages

- Most widely used method.
    
- Suitable for hydrophobic drugs.
    

---

## 2. Double Emulsion Method (W/O/W)

### Principle

Suitable for hydrophilic drugs and proteins.

### Procedure

1. Drug dissolved in aqueous phase.
    
2. Emulsified into polymer solution.
    
3. Secondary emulsification performed.
    
4. Solvent evaporated.
    
5. Microspheres formed.
    

### Application

- Proteins
    
- Peptides
    
- Vaccines
    

---

## 3. Spray Drying Method

### Procedure

1. Drug-polymer solution prepared.
    
2. Atomized into hot air chamber.
    
3. Solvent evaporates rapidly.
    
4. Dry microspheres collected.
    

### Advantages

- Simple
    
- Industrially scalable
    
- Continuous process
    

---

## 4. Hot Melt / Spray Congealing Method

### Procedure

1. Polymer or wax melted.
    
2. Drug dispersed in melt.
    
3. Atomized into cool air.
    
4. Microspheres solidify.
    

### Advantage

- Solvent-free process.
    

---

## 5. Ionic Gelation Method

### Principle

Cross-linking of polymers using ions.

### Examples

- Alginate + Calcium chloride
    
- Chitosan + TPP
    

### Advantages

- Mild conditions
    
- Suitable for proteins and peptides
    

---

## 6. Polymerization Method

### Types

- Suspension polymerization
    
- Emulsion polymerization
    

### Principle

Polymerization of monomers around drug molecules to form microspheres.

---

# Evaluation of Microspheres

## 1. Particle Size Analysis

### Methods

- Optical microscopy
    
- Laser diffraction
    
- Coulter counter
    

### Importance

Affects drug release and targeting efficiency.

---

## 2. Morphology

### Methods

- SEM (Scanning Electron Microscopy)
    
- TEM (Transmission Electron Microscopy)
    

### Purpose

Determines shape and surface structure.

---

## 3. Drug Content

### Purpose

Determines actual amount of drug present.

### Method

Drug extraction followed by UV or HPLC analysis.

---

## 4. Encapsulation Efficiency (EE%)

### Formula

[  
EE% = \frac{Entrapped\ Drug}{Total\ Drug} \times 100  
]

### Purpose

Determines efficiency of encapsulation.

---

## 5. Polymer Molecular Weight

### Method

Gel Permeation Chromatography (GPC)

### Importance

Influences degradation and drug release rate.

---

## 6. Thermal Analysis

### Methods

- DSC (Differential Scanning Calorimetry)
    
- TGA (Thermogravimetric Analysis)
    

### Purpose

Determines thermal stability and drug-polymer interaction.

---

## 7. In-vitro Drug Release Study

### Methods

- USP Dissolution Apparatus
    
- Sample and Separate Method
    

### Purpose

Evaluates release profile and release kinetics.

---

## 8. Stability Studies

### Evaluate

- Drug content
    
- Particle size
    
- Encapsulation efficiency
    
- Release profile
    

### Purpose

Determines shelf life and stability.

---

## 9. Biological Evaluation

### In-vitro

- Cytotoxicity studies
    
- Cellular uptake studies
    

### In-vivo

- Pharmacokinetics
    
- Biodistribution
    
- Efficacy studies
    

### Purpose

Evaluates safety and therapeutic performance.

---

# Advantages of Microspheres

- Controlled and sustained drug release
    
- Improved bioavailability
    
- Reduced dosing frequency
    
- Targeted drug delivery
    
- Reduced toxicity
    
- Improved patient compliance
    
- Protection of drugs from degradation
    

---

# Limitations

- Initial burst release
    
- High production cost
    
- Scale-up difficulties
    
- Stability issues
    
- Complex manufacturing process
    

---

# Conclusion

Microspheres are monolithic matrix drug delivery systems in which the drug is uniformly distributed throughout a polymer matrix. Various types such as biodegradable, non-biodegradable, bioadhesive, floating, magnetic, and radioactive microspheres are prepared using solvent evaporation, double emulsion, spray drying, hot melt, ionic gelation, and polymerization methods. Their evaluation includes particle size, morphology, drug content, encapsulation efficiency, drug release studies, stability testing, and biological evaluation. Microspheres are widely used for controlled and targeted drug delivery.

# Most Important Difference (Exam Favorite)

|Microcapsules|Microspheres|
|---|---|
|Core-shell structure|Matrix structure|
|Drug present in core|Drug dispersed throughout matrix|
|Has distinct coating wall|No distinct wall|
|Release through membrane|Release through diffusion/erosion|
|Example: Coated microcapsules|Example: PLGA microspheres|
