# TARGETING METHODS

## Introduction, Preparation and Evaluation

# Introduction

Targeting methods are advanced drug delivery approaches used to deliver drugs specifically to the diseased site while minimizing drug distribution to normal tissues. The main aim is to improve therapeutic efficacy and reduce systemic toxicity. These methods exploit biological, physiological, or anatomical differences between diseased and healthy tissues.

Conventional drug delivery suffers from:

- Non-specific distribution
    
- Rapid drug clearance
    
- Biological barriers
    
- Multidrug resistance
    

Targeting methods overcome these limitations by selective drug localization.

---

# Classification of Targeting Methods

## 1. Passive Targeting

Uses pathological conditions such as the **Enhanced Permeability and Retention (EPR) effect** for drug accumulation.

## 2. Active Targeting

Uses ligands or antibodies that bind specifically to receptors on target cells.

## 3. Physical Targeting

Uses external stimuli such as:

- Magnetic field
    
- Ultrasound
    
- Heat
    
- Light
    

## 4. Biological Targeting

Uses biological carriers such as:

- Cells
    
- Exosomes
    
- Antibodies
    

---

# Preparation of Targeting Systems

Preparation depends on the type of targeting system used.

---

# 1. Preparation of Passive Targeting Systems

Passive targeting systems are mainly prepared by controlling:

- Particle size
    
- Surface charge
    
- Surface modification
    

## A. Size Engineering

Optimal particle size:

- 50–200 nm
    

### Importance

- Avoids renal clearance
    
- Avoids rapid uptake by macrophages
    
- Enhances EPR-mediated tumor accumulation
    

### Methods for Size Control

- Sonication
    
- Homogenization
    
- Membrane extrusion
    
- Emulsification techniques
    

---

## B. PEGylation

PEGylation means attachment of **Polyethylene Glycol (PEG)** to the carrier surface.

### Advantages

- Increases circulation time
    
- Reduces opsonization
    
- Avoids macrophage uptake
    
- Produces stealth nanoparticles
    

### Example

PEGylated liposomes.

---

## C. Charge Optimization

Surface charge affects:

- Stability
    
- Cellular interaction
    
- Protein adsorption
    

Optimal zeta potential:

- −20 to −30 mV
    

This improves colloidal stability and reduces aggregation.

---

# 2. Preparation of Active Targeting Systems

Active targeting involves attachment of ligands to carrier surfaces.

## Ligands Used

- Antibodies
    
- Peptides
    
- Vitamins
    
- Aptamers
    
- Proteins
    

### Examples

- Folic acid
    
- Transferrin
    
- Trastuzumab
    
- RGD peptide
    

---

## Ligand Conjugation Methods

### A. Carbodiimide Coupling (EDC/NHS)

Used for coupling:

- Carboxyl groups (–COOH)
    
- Amino groups (–NH₂)
    

Forms stable amide bonds.

---

### B. Maleimide-Thiol Chemistry

Used for conjugation between:

- Thiol groups (–SH)
    
- Maleimide groups
    

Produces stable thioether linkage.

---

### C. Click Chemistry

Uses azide-alkyne cycloaddition.

### Advantages

- Highly specific
    
- Efficient
    
- Bioorthogonal reaction
    

---

# 3. Preparation of Physical Targeting Systems

## Magnetic Targeting

Uses:

- Superparamagnetic iron oxide nanoparticles (SPIONs)
    

### Preparation

- Co-precipitation method
    
- Thermal decomposition method
    

Drug is loaded onto magnetic nanoparticles and guided using an external magnetic field.

---

## Thermosensitive Systems

Prepared using:

- Thermosensitive liposomes
    
- Thermosensitive polymers
    

Drug release occurs at elevated temperature (40–42°C).

---

# 4. Preparation of Biological Targeting Systems

## Cell-Mediated Delivery

Drug-loaded nanoparticles are incorporated into:

- Macrophages
    
- Red blood cells
    
- Stem cells
    

These cells naturally migrate to diseased tissue.

---

## Exosome-Based Targeting

Exosomes are isolated from cells and loaded with drugs or nucleic acids.

### Advantages

- Biocompatible
    
- Natural targeting ability
    
- Low immunogenicity
    

---

# Evaluation of Targeting Methods

Evaluation confirms whether selective targeting is achieved.

---

# 1. In Vitro Evaluation

## A. Particle Size and Zeta Potential

Measured using:

- Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS)
    

### Purpose

- Determines particle stability and targeting suitability.
    

---

## B. Morphology

Measured using:

- Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM)
    
- Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM)
    

### Purpose

- Determines shape and surface characteristics.
    

---

## C. Drug Loading and Entrapment Efficiency

Measured using:

- HPLC
    
- UV spectroscopy
    

### Purpose

- Determines amount of drug encapsulated.
    

---

## D. In Vitro Drug Release Study

Performed using:

- Dialysis bag method
    
- Franz diffusion cell
    

### Purpose

- Evaluates controlled and targeted drug release.
    

---

## E. Cellular Uptake Studies

Methods:

- Flow cytometry
    
- Confocal microscopy
    

### Purpose

- Measures uptake by target cells.
    

---

## F. Cytotoxicity Study

Methods:

- MTT assay
    
- SRB assay
    

### Purpose

- Determines anticancer activity and selectivity.
    

---

# 2. In Vivo Evaluation

## A. Biodistribution Study

Uses:

- Radiolabeled or fluorescent carriers
    

### Purpose

- Determines drug accumulation in target organs.
    

---

## B. Pharmacokinetic Studies

Parameters:

- Half-life (t½)
    
- Area under curve (AUC)
    
- Clearance
    

### Purpose

- Evaluates circulation and targeting efficiency.
    

---

## C. Tumor Growth Inhibition Study

### Purpose

- Measures therapeutic effectiveness in tumor models.
    

---

## D. Imaging Techniques

Methods:

- PET
    
- MRI
    
- SPECT
    
- Fluorescence imaging
    

### Purpose

- Visualizes drug localization in vivo.
    

---

## E. Safety Evaluation

Includes:

- Histopathology
    
- Liver function tests
    
- Kidney function tests
    
- Hematological studies
    

### Purpose

- Evaluates systemic toxicity.
    

---

# Ideal Properties of Targeting Systems

An ideal targeting system should possess:

- High targeting efficiency
    
- Biocompatibility
    
- Biodegradability
    
- Stability
    
- Controlled drug release
    
- Adequate drug loading
    
- Scalability for industrial production
    

---

# Advantages of Targeting Methods

- Site-specific drug delivery
    
- Reduced toxicity
    
- Improved therapeutic efficacy
    
- Increased bioavailability
    
- Reduced dosing frequency
    
- Better patient compliance
    

---

# Limitations

- Complex preparation methods
    
- High manufacturing cost
    
- Stability problems
    
- Protein corona formation
    
- Difficulty in clinical translation
    

---

# Conclusion

Targeting methods are advanced drug delivery strategies that improve selective delivery of drugs to diseased tissues using passive, active, physical, and biological approaches. Proper preparation and detailed evaluation of targeting systems are essential to achieve efficient drug localization, improved therapeutic efficacy, and reduced systemic toxicity.
