# ELECTROSOMES

## Preparation and Applications (10 Marks)

# Introduction

Electrosomes are vesicular drug delivery systems (similar to liposomes or niosomes) in which **surface charge is the key factor responsible for drug targeting and cellular uptake**. The vesicles may possess either:

- Positive charge (Cationic electrosomes)
    
- Negative charge (Anionic electrosomes)
    

The charged surface helps interaction with biological membranes, DNA, RNA, and mucosal surfaces, making electrosomes useful for gene delivery and targeted drug delivery.

---

# Types of Electrosomes

## 1. Cationic Electrosomes (+)

Contain positively charged lipids such as:

- DOTAP
    
- DOTMA
    
- DC-Chol
    
- DDAB
    
- Stearylamine
    

### Applications

- Gene delivery
    
- DNA delivery
    
- mRNA delivery
    
- Cancer therapy
    

---

## 2. Anionic Electrosomes (−)

Contain negatively charged lipids such as:

- Dicetyl phosphate (DCP)
    
- Phosphatidic acid
    
- Phosphatidylglycerol
    

### Applications

- Liver targeting
    
- Anti-inflammatory drug delivery
    
- Pulmonary drug delivery
    

---

# Preparation of Electrosomes

## 1. Thin Film Hydration Method (Most Common)

### Procedure

1. Phospholipids, cholesterol, and charged lipid dissolved in chloroform:methanol.
    
2. Organic solvent removed using rotary evaporator.
    
3. Thin lipid film formed.
    
4. Hydration with aqueous phase.
    
5. Multilamellar vesicles formed.
    
6. Sonication or extrusion performed to reduce size.
    

### Example

DOTAP + DOPE + Cholesterol

↓

Cationic Electrosomes

---

## 2. Preparation of Cationic Lipoplexes

### Procedure

1. Prepare cationic electrosomes.
    
2. Mix with DNA, RNA, siRNA or plasmid.
    
3. Electrostatic interaction occurs.
    
4. Lipoplex complex formed.
    

### Importance

- Protects genetic material.
    
- Enhances cellular uptake.
    

---

## 3. Microfluidic Method

### Procedure

1. Ionizable lipid dissolved in ethanol.
    
2. mRNA/siRNA dissolved in acidic aqueous buffer.
    
3. Both streams mixed in microfluidic chamber.
    
4. Self-assembly occurs.
    
5. Electrosomes formed.
    

### Advantages

- Uniform particle size
    
- Suitable for mRNA vaccines
    

---

# Flow Chart

```text
Charged Lipid + Phospholipid + Cholesterol
                    ↓
      Dissolve in Organic Solvent
                    ↓
           Thin Film Formation
                    ↓
               Hydration
                    ↓
         Electrosome Formation
                    ↓
        Sonication / Extrusion
                    ↓
           Final Electrosomes
```

# Applications of Electrosomes

## 1. Gene Delivery (Most Important)

Used for delivery of:

- DNA
    
- RNA
    
- siRNA
    
- mRNA
    

### Advantage

- Protects nucleic acids.
    
- Improves transfection efficiency.
    

---

## 2. mRNA Vaccines

### Examples

- Pfizer COVID-19 Vaccine
    
- Moderna COVID-19 Vaccine
    

Electrosomes deliver mRNA into cells for protein expression.

---

## 3. Cancer Therapy

Used for delivery of:

- Tumor suppressor genes
    
- Anticancer drugs
    
- siRNA against oncogenes
    

### Benefits

- Targeted delivery
    
- Reduced toxicity
    

---

## 4. Ophthalmic Drug Delivery

### Benefits

- Better corneal adhesion
    
- Increased residence time
    
- Improved ocular bioavailability
    

---

## 5. Nasal Drug Delivery

### Benefits

- Mucoadhesion
    
- Improved absorption
    
- Nose-to-brain delivery
    

---

## 6. Vaginal and Rectal Drug Delivery

Used for:

- Anti-HIV formulations
    
- Sustained local drug release
    

---

## 7. Antimicrobial Drug Delivery

Used for delivery of:

- Ciprofloxacin
    
- Vancomycin
    

### Benefits

- Enhanced interaction with bacterial membranes.
    
- Improved antibacterial activity.
    

---

## Advantages

- Targeted drug delivery
    
- Enhanced cellular uptake
    
- Effective gene delivery
    
- Improved bioavailability
    
- Controlled drug release
    
- Suitable for nucleic acid delivery
    

---

## Limitations

- Cationic lipid toxicity
    
- Stability issues
    
- High manufacturing cost
    
- Possible immune reactions
    

---

# Conclusion

Electrosomes are charged vesicular drug delivery systems that use electrostatic interactions for enhanced drug and gene delivery. They are prepared mainly by thin film hydration, lipoplex formation, and microfluidic methods. Their major applications include gene therapy, mRNA vaccines, cancer therapy, ophthalmic, nasal, and antimicrobial drug delivery.
