
## Types, Preparation and Evaluation

# Introduction

Nanoparticles are solid colloidal drug carriers with a size range of **1–1000 nm**. They improve drug delivery by enhancing bioavailability, increasing therapeutic efficacy, reducing toxicity, and enabling targeted drug delivery. Nanoparticles can protect drugs from degradation and provide controlled drug release.

---

# Types of Nanoparticles

## 1. Polymeric Nanoparticles

Prepared from natural or synthetic polymers.

### Types

### A. Nanospheres

- Drug uniformly dispersed throughout polymer matrix.
    
- Matrix-type system.
    

### B. Nanocapsules

- Core-shell structure.
    
- Drug enclosed within a cavity surrounded by polymer membrane.
    

### Examples

- PLGA nanoparticles
    
- PLA nanoparticles
    
- Chitosan nanoparticles
    
- Albumin nanoparticles
    

---

## 2. Solid Lipid Nanoparticles (SLN)

- Made from solid lipids.
    
- Drug dispersed in solid lipid matrix.
    

### Advantages

- Biocompatible
    
- Controlled release
    
- Improved stability
    

### Examples

- Glyceryl monostearate
    
- Cetyl palmitate based nanoparticles
    

---

## 3. Nanostructured Lipid Carriers (NLC)

- Prepared from mixture of solid and liquid lipids.
    
- Higher drug loading than SLNs.
    
- Better physical stability.
    

---

## 4. Inorganic Nanoparticles

### Gold Nanoparticles

- Used in photothermal therapy.
    

### SPIONs

(**Superparamagnetic Iron Oxide Nanoparticles**)

- Used for magnetic targeting.
    

### Mesoporous Silica Nanoparticles

- High drug loading capacity.
    

### Quantum Dots

- Used in imaging and diagnostics.
    

---

## 5. Dendrimers

- Highly branched polymeric nanoparticles.
    
- High drug loading capability.
    
- Used in gene and drug delivery.
    

---

# Preparation of Nanoparticles

## 1. Nanoprecipitation Method

### Principle

Polymer and drug are dissolved in organic solvent and added to aqueous phase.

### Procedure

1. Dissolve polymer and drug in organic solvent.
    
2. Add solution to aqueous phase containing stabilizer.
    
3. Polymer precipitates as nanoparticles.
    
4. Remove solvent by evaporation.
    

### Advantages

- Simple method
    
- Uniform particle size
    
- Suitable for hydrophobic drugs
    

---

## 2. Emulsification-Solvent Evaporation Method

### Procedure

1. Polymer and drug dissolved in organic solvent.
    
2. Emulsified in aqueous phase.
    
3. Solvent evaporated.
    
4. Nanoparticles formed.
    

### Types

- Single emulsion (o/w)
    
- Double emulsion (w/o/w)
    

### Applications

- Hydrophobic drugs
    
- Proteins and peptides
    

---

## 3. Salting-Out Method

### Principle

High salt concentration prevents mixing of solvent and water.

### Procedure

1. Prepare polymer solution.
    
2. Emulsify in aqueous salt solution.
    
3. Add water.
    
4. Polymer precipitates forming nanoparticles.
    

### Advantage

Suitable for sensitive drugs and proteins.

---

## 4. High Pressure Homogenization (SLN/NLC)

### Procedure

1. Melt lipid.
    
2. Add drug.
    
3. Emulsify with surfactant solution.
    
4. Homogenize under high pressure.
    
5. Cool to form nanoparticles.
    

### Advantage

Large-scale production possible.

---

# Evaluation of Nanoparticles

## 1. Particle Size Analysis

### Method

Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS)

### Importance

- Determines particle size distribution.
    
- Affects targeting and stability.
    

---

## 2. Zeta Potential

### Importance

Measures surface charge.

### Significance

- Indicates stability.
    
- High zeta potential prevents aggregation.
    

Optimal value:  
±30 mV or more.

---

## 3. Morphology

### Methods

- Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM)
    
- Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM)
    

### Purpose

Determines particle shape and structure.

---

## 4. Drug Entrapment Efficiency (EE%)

### Formula

[  
EE% = \frac{Total\ Drug - Free\ Drug}{Total\ Drug} \times 100  
]

### Purpose

Determines amount of drug encapsulated.

---

## 5. Drug Loading (DL%)

### Formula

[  
DL% = \frac{Encapsulated\ Drug}{Total\ Nanoparticle\ Weight} \times 100  
]

### Purpose

Determines drug content in nanoparticles.

---

## 6. In-Vitro Drug Release Study

### Method

Dialysis Bag Method

### Purpose

Evaluates release profile and controlled release behavior.

---

## 7. Stability Studies

Evaluate:

- Particle size
    
- Zeta potential
    
- Drug content
    
- Aggregation
    

Purpose:  
To determine shelf-life and formulation stability.

---

## 8. Biological Evaluation

### In-vitro

- Cellular uptake studies
    
- Cytotoxicity studies (MTT assay)
    

### In-vivo

- Biodistribution studies
    
- Pharmacokinetic studies
    
- Tumor growth inhibition studies
    
- Safety studies
    

---

# Advantages of Nanoparticles

- Targeted drug delivery
    
- Controlled drug release
    
- Reduced toxicity
    
- Improved bioavailability
    
- Protection of drugs from degradation
    
- Enhanced therapeutic efficacy
    

---

# Limitations

- High production cost
    
- Scale-up difficulties
    
- Stability issues
    
- Protein corona formation
    
- Regulatory challenges
    

---

# Conclusion

Nanoparticles are versatile drug delivery systems capable of improving drug targeting, bioavailability, and therapeutic efficacy. Various types such as polymeric nanoparticles, SLNs, NLCs, dendrimers, and inorganic nanoparticles are prepared using methods like nanoprecipitation, emulsification, salting-out, and homogenization. Their performance is evaluated by particle size, zeta potential, morphology, drug loading, release studies, and biological evaluation.
