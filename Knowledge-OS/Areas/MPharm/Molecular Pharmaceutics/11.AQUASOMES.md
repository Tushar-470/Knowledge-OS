# AQUASOMES

## Preparation and Applications (10 Marks Answer)

# Introduction

Aquasomes are **three-layered nanoparticulate carrier systems** specially designed for the delivery of proteins, peptides, enzymes, antigens, and genes while preserving their biological activity and three-dimensional structure.

The term **Aquasome** comes from:

- **Aqua** = Water
    
- **Soma** = Body
    

Aquasomes work on the **Water Replacement Theory**, where carbohydrate molecules replace water around proteins and maintain their native structure during storage and delivery.

---

# Structure of Aquasomes

Aquasomes consist of three layers:

### 1. Ceramic Core

Examples:

- Hydroxyapatite
    
- Calcium phosphate
    
- Tin oxide
    

Function:

- Provides structural support.
    
- Protects the drug.
    

### 2. Carbohydrate Coating

Examples:

- Trehalose
    
- Cellobiose
    
- Sucrose
    

Function:

- Preserves protein structure.
    
- Prevents denaturation.
    

### 3. Drug/Protein Layer

Examples:

- Insulin
    
- Hemoglobin
    
- Enzymes
    
- Antigens
    

Function:

- Therapeutic action.
    

---

# Preparation of Aquasomes

Aquasome preparation occurs in **three stages**.

---

## Stage 1: Preparation of Ceramic Core

### Procedure

1. Ceramic nanoparticles prepared by co-precipitation method.
    
2. Commonly hydroxyapatite nanoparticles are synthesized.
    
3. Particles are filtered, washed and dried.
    
4. Nanoparticles of size 60–300 nm are obtained.
    

### Purpose

- Provides rigid and stable core.
    
- Large surface area for drug loading.
    

---

## Stage 2: Carbohydrate Coating

### Procedure

1. Ceramic nanoparticles dispersed in water.
    
2. Carbohydrate solution (trehalose, cellobiose, sucrose) prepared.
    
3. Nanoparticles mixed with carbohydrate solution.
    
4. Carbohydrate self-assembles onto ceramic surface.
    
5. Excess carbohydrate removed by centrifugation.
    

### Purpose

- Protects protein structure.
    
- Mimics hydration layer around proteins.
    

---

## Stage 3: Drug/Protein Adsorption

### Procedure

1. Protein or drug solution added to carbohydrate-coated nanoparticles.
    
2. Gentle stirring carried out at low temperature (4°C).
    
3. Protein adsorbs onto carbohydrate layer through hydrogen bonding.
    
4. Unbound protein removed by centrifugation.
    
5. Aquasomes collected and stored.
    

### Purpose

- Maintains biological activity.
    
- Avoids protein denaturation.
    

---

# Flow Chart (Very Important)

```text
Ceramic Core Preparation
(Hydroxyapatite Nanoparticles)
              ↓
Carbohydrate Coating
(Trehalose / Cellobiose / Sucrose)
              ↓
Protein Adsorption
(Insulin / Hemoglobin / Antigen)
              ↓
         AQUASOMES
```

# Applications of Aquasomes

## 1. Protein Drug Delivery

Aquasomes protect proteins from denaturation.

### Examples

- Insulin
    
- Erythropoietin
    
- Interferons
    

---

## 2. Peptide Drug Delivery

Improve stability and bioavailability of peptides.

### Examples

- Calcitonin
    
- Parathyroid hormone
    

---

## 3. Enzyme Delivery

Protect enzymes and maintain activity.

### Examples

- Catalase
    
- Alkaline phosphatase
    
- Glucose oxidase
    

---

## 4. Vaccine Delivery

Used as carriers for antigens.

### Advantages

- Enhanced immune response.
    
- Improved antigen stability.
    

### Examples

- Hepatitis B antigen
    
- Malaria antigens
    

---

## 5. Hemoglobin Delivery

Used as artificial oxygen carriers.

### Advantages

- Maintains hemoglobin structure.
    
- Preserves oxygen-carrying capacity.
    

### Application

Artificial blood substitutes.

---

## 6. Gene Delivery

Used for delivery of:

- DNA
    
- RNA
    
- Oligonucleotides
    

---

## 7. Oral Delivery of Proteins

Protect proteins from degradation in GIT.

### Example

- Oral insulin delivery
    

---

## 8. Targeted Drug Delivery

Deliver drugs to specific tissues with reduced side effects.

---

# Advantages

- Excellent biocompatibility
    
- Preserve protein conformation
    
- High stability
    
- Protect against degradation
    
- Controlled drug release
    
- Targeted delivery possible
    
- Suitable for proteins and peptides
    

---

# Limitations

- Complex preparation process
    
- Low drug loading capacity
    
- Physical instability in suspension
    
- Limited commercial products
    

---

# Conclusion

Aquasomes are three-layered nanoparticulate drug carriers consisting of a **ceramic core, carbohydrate coating, and protein layer**. They are prepared by ceramic core formation, carbohydrate coating, and protein adsorption. Aquasomes are particularly useful for the delivery of proteins, peptides, enzymes, vaccines, hemoglobin, and genes because they preserve biological activity through the water replacement mechanism.
