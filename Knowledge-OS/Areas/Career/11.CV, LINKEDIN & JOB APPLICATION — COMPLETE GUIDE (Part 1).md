> [!IMPORTANT]

> **ATS (Applicant Tracking System)** scans your CV before any human sees it. >75% of resumes are rejected by ATS before reaching a recruiter. Rules: (1) Use simple formatting — NO tables, columns, graphics, headers/footers, or text boxes. (2) Use standard section headings. (3) Match keywords from the job posting EXACTLY. (4) Save as .docx (preferred by most ATS) or .pdf.

### How to Write Bullet Points — STAR-A Method

  

**S**ituation → **T**ask → **A**ction → **R**esult → **A**dded value

  

| ❌ WEAK Bullet | ✅ STRONG Bullet |

|:---|:---|

| "Performed HPLC analysis" | "Developed and validated a stability-indicating RP-HPLC method (C18, 233nm) per ICH Q2(R2) with linearity r² = 0.9999 and accuracy 99.2–100.8%, enabling reliable impurity profiling of Metformin HCl SR tablets" |

| "Did dissolution testing" | "Established dissolution similarity with innovator product (Glucophage XR, f2 = 64) using USP Apparatus II (50 RPM, pH 6.8 phosphate buffer), supporting ANDA-ready documentation" |

| "Used DoE for optimization" | "Applied 3² full factorial DoE (Design-Expert) to optimize HPMC:EC ratio, identifying a design space of HPMC 28–35% / EC 15–22% that consistently delivered zero-order release over 12 hours (R² = 0.9945)" |

| "Conducted stability studies" | "Executed 6-month accelerated stability study (40°C/75%RH) per ICH Q1A(R2) across 3 batches — no significant change in assay (99.1–100.4%), degradation products (<0.2%), or dissolution profile" |

| "Did preformulation studies" | "Performed comprehensive preformulation characterization including DSC (Tm = 224°C), FTIR (functional group confirmation), XRPD (Form I confirmed), and drug-excipient compatibility (binary mixtures at 40°C/75%RH, 4 weeks)" |

# TASK 2 — TOP 20 CV MISTAKES (WITH CORRECTIONS)

# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  

| # | ❌ MISTAKE | ✅ CORRECTION |

|:--|:---|:---|

| 1 | **Generic objective statement** ("Seeking a challenging role...") | Replace with a **Professional Summary** (3 lines) citing specific skills: HPLC, DoE, ICH Q1A |

| 2 | **Listing duties instead of achievements** ("Responsible for lab work") | Use STAR-A bullet format: action + method + result + metric |

| 3 | **No quantifiable results** | Add numbers: f2 = 64, r² = 0.9999, RSD 0.6%, 12-hour release, 3 batches |

| 4 | **Using tables/columns** in CV | ATS cannot parse tables. Use simple single-column text format |

| 5 | **Photo on USA resume** | NEVER include photo for USA applications. Legal issue. OK for EU (optional) and India |

| 6 | **Including personal details on USA resume** | No DOB, religion, marital status, father's name. Only: name, phone, email, LinkedIn, city |

| 7 | **Unprofessional email** (coolboy123@gmail.com) | Use firstname.lastname@gmail.com |

| 8 | **3+ pages for a fresher** | India: max 2 pages. USA: strictly 1 page. EU: max 2 pages |

| 9 | **Listing every subject studied** | Only mention coursework if directly relevant to the job (e.g., "Coursework: Biopharmaceutics, Industrial Pharmacy, Pharmaceutical Analysis") |

| 10 | **Not including thesis title** | Your thesis IS your primary experience. Give it a dedicated section with 4–6 detailed bullets |

| 11 | **Spelling/grammar errors** | In pharma, attention to detail = patient safety. A typo suggests carelessness. Proofread 3× |

| 12 | **Not tailoring to job description** | Read the JD. If they say "dissolution testing" → use "dissolution testing" (not "drug release study"). Mirror their language |

| 13 | **Listing instruments without context** | ❌ "HPLC, UV, DSC". ✅ "HPLC (Waters Alliance e2695 — method development, validation, routine analysis)" |

| 14 | **No LinkedIn URL** | Add your customized LinkedIn URL (linkedin.com/in/yourname) to the header |

| 15 | **Fancy fonts/colors/borders** | Use Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman. Size 10–11pt. Black text. No colors (ATS can't read colored text in some systems) |

| 16 | **Including references on CV** | Write "Available upon request" or omit entirely. Don't waste space listing references |

| 17 | **Including hobbies unrelated to pharma** | ❌ "Cricket, cooking, travelling". ✅ Only include if relevant: "Active member of Indian Pharmaceutical Association (IPA)" |

| 18 | **Not mentioning GPAT score/rank** | For Indian applications, GPAT qualification shows aptitude. Include score and AIR |

| 19 | **Using passive voice** | ❌ "Stability studies were performed by me." ✅ "Executed ICH Q1A stability studies across 3 batches" — active, direct, strong |

| 20 | **Sending same CV to formulation and analytical roles** | Customize: For **formulation roles**, lead with DoE, excipient selection, dissolution. For **analytical roles**, lead with HPLC, method validation, forced degradation |

### Cold Outreach Message Templates

  

**Template 1: To a Recruiter (India)**

> "Dear [Name], I am a final-year M.Pharm (Pharmaceutics) student actively seeking R&D Scientist positions. > "Dear [Name], I came across your profile and was very impressed by your work in [specific area] at [Company]. As a final-year M.Pharm student specializing in formulation development, I am actively exploring R&D Scientist opportunities. I would be grateful for any advice on how to best position my application for [Company], or if you would be open to a brief 10-minute conversation about your experience in the role. **Template 3: To a USA/EU Recruiter (International)**

> "Dear [Name], I am an M.Pharm (Pharmaceutics) graduate from India with research experience in QbD-based formulation development, ICH-compliant HPLC method validation, and dissolution method development. I am seeking entry-level R&D Scientist opportunities in [USA/EU] and would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my skills align with positions at [Company]. > **Key Outreach Rules:**

> - Personalize EVERY message — mention something specific about the person or company

> - Keep it under 100 words for connection requests

> - Follow up after 1 week if no response (once only — never spam)

> - Connect on Tuesday–Thursday mornings for highest response rates

> - Post 1–2 times per week (share articles, comment on industry news) to stay visible
